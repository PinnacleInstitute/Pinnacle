Real-World Promotions - 5 Great Ways To Promote Your Lead Program Offline.


Method #1.   Flyer Distribution Done Anonymously

 Flyers distributed in the right manner can be an effective way of collecting local leads.  This is a very simple 8 1/2" x 11" flyer with tear-offs across the bottom. The process is quite simple - describe the key points of the business.  You can usually just copy this from your lead program. 

On the flyer allow some room at the bottom for you to cut "tear off" strips and on those strips include your lead program URL. 

  • Grocery store bulletin boards
  • Community center bulletin boards
  • Phone booths
  • College bulletin boards
  • Laundromat bulletin boards
  • Anywhere you can legally pin or tape one!

Like any advertising method you need to commit yourself.  If you were to put up just 10 flyers around your town in high traffic locations you will most likely develop some positive response. 

 
Method #2.  Traditional Business Cards Work Too!

Most traditional businesses that have a website place their website URL on almost all of the stationary that leaves their office.  So should you.  If you have business cards that your distributing try placing your lead program URL on the bottom.  A lead program is a much softer sell  then sending them directly to your main company website.  At least this way you know they are truly interested because they will submit their information.

 

Method #3.  Prospecting (Sizzle) Cards!  

A Sizzle Card is about the size of a traditional business card and contains a provocative headline and a low impact way of gaining more information.
 
If you take a quick trip to Google you can find many companies that will print these for around $25.00 for a 1,000 cards. A great deal to be sure.

Now, what do you do with these Sizzle Cards? 
 
Get them in as many hands as possible! Put them everywhere you can. Distribute them any way you can think of. Here are some suggestions that are very successfully:

  • One-on-one quick contact with everyone 
  • Bank machines 
  • Food court table at the mall 
  • People at a bus stop 
  • In a small holder at the health club front desk 
  • Taxi/bus driver 
  • Restaurant wait staff 
  • In return envelopes with bill payments 
  • Bulletin boards that allow business card posts

The sky's the limit! Use your imagination

 

Method #4.   How Many Miles Do You Drive?

How often do you see a car or truck with some form of corporate identity displayed?  Probably all the time.  You also probably always read what their slogan is or see if they have a website address.  Wouldn't you like it if people were this curious about your business opportunity?  Well then give them the chance.  And get your website address put onto your car with your own special slogan.  People can write down the address or remember it if it's easy and submit their info through your lead program.  You can also write off a portion of your car, gas and insurance as a business expense.  Win/Win.

Method #5.   Do You Read Any Local Papers?

You probably already know that other people read them too. Small local papers with strong readership and special weekly papers like the Nickel Nick or Penny Saver type publications can be very cost effective advertising.

You are not limited to just the local papers in your own area thanks to the Internet you can search out local papers from towns in your state or local area that you might want to place ads in. Typically, the Business Opportunity/Business Franchise section works well but you are only limited by your own creativity!

You don't have to be an advertising genius either.

Here's an ad that gets the most response for real clients. It's a simple two line ad that most people would never have written or wasted money advertising.

Here's the magic two-line ad:

A 100% True Home-Based Business
www.WebSite.com - (555) 555-1212

It just goes to show you that effective advertising doesn't always follow any kind of rhyme or reason.

Here are some important facts about this ad...

1) Every time this ad ran in the local area it received between 10 and 30 replies.

2) Most people went to the web site, they didn't call. There's no chance that they're going to talk to a pushy salesman.

3) The web site was a very targeted lead program with the opportunity for the prospect to directly respond and ask to be contacted.
