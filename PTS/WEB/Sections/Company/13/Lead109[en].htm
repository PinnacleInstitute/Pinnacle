How do I find the right business for me?

You can find the opportunity that’s best for you by conducting your own business analysis.  Professional investors always examine three important factors, the People, the Product, and the Return on Investment (ROI).  You too should carefully evaluate these critical factors to insure that your emotional, financial and time resources are optimally invested. 

My friend told me they’re a great company, but…

What should I really be looking for?

Carefully research the Company’s past performance and the background of the founders.  The people are the most important factor.  Do whatever it takes to determine two key things – are they trustworthy and are they competent?  The management team must have integrity and be willing to always tell the truth regardless off how difficult the situation. They must be experienced and capable of managing the tactical and strategic business objectives.

  I’d love to find the next Hoola-hoop or Pet Rock, but…

How can I tell if the product has real potential?

Product potential always starts with quality and value. The product must be of high quality to generate excitement for the product by the customers and distributors.  The product must have retail value to outside individuals for retail sale. Don’t forget about demand – the product should have mass-market appeal to provide a broad potential customer base.  

I know I’m going to have to work hard, but…

How can I be sure I’ll make money?

Confirm that the compensation plan is based on actual product sales and not on just recruiting or money games. The business must attract distributors that are willing to work hard for a personally rewarding experience.  It must not be based on “free money” or “no work required” promises that only attract non-workers and get-rich-quick junkies.  The business must be relatively easy for most distributors to earn $300 - $500 per month and it should be possible to earn $50,000+ monthly for the motivated sales leaders and dream builders.
