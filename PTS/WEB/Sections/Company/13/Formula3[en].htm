Share the Products and Opportunity

To understand how to market your products and opportunity, you must first understand the purpose of business.  Unfortunately, many people have the misunderstanding that the purpose of business is to make money.  This couldn’t be further from the truth.  Money is a byproduct of successfully providing a solution to someone’s problem. 

“The purpose of business is to solve problems.”

Once you understand this principle, you will then understand that marketing is all about figuring out:

  • What problems you have a solution for
  • What kinds of people have these problems
  • Where these people are and how to reach them
  • How to get their attention and interest.

You will also learn that business is all about building and maintaining relationships.  The majority of your time should be involved in relationship building activities.  You must leverage tools to assist you in effectively managing the mundane administrative tasks of running a business so that you can focus on relationship building.

Marketing Sequence
Successful marketing recognizes a sequence of steps that must be followed.  Each step defines how to accomplish a specific objective that leads to the next step.  

  • Identify Your Target Market   (analysis)
  • Get in Front of Them   (promotion)
  • Get Their Attention   (message)
  • Get Their Interest   (lead program)

Unqualified Prospects
New prospects that give you their attention, but have not yet expressed their interest are unqualified prospects.  You will want to share an appropriate lead program with your unqualified prospects to see if they are interested.  The primary objective of the lead program is to get the prospect to say "That's interesting, I'd like to know more".

Qualified Prospects 
After a prospect has expressed interest they are now qualified and ready to be converted to a customer or partner via the sales process.  There is no need to share a lead program with them, because its objective has already been accomplished.
