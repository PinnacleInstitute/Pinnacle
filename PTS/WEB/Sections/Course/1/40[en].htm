You can create your own Custom Lead Pages to attract interested prospects.


 

Lead Pages

A list of all your Custom Lead Pages will be displayed for your review and editing.  The Lead Page status is displayed on the right side of the list.  You can click the Preview, Traffic and Usage icons in the middle of the list to review the corresponding functionality of your Custom Lead Pages.  You can click the pencil  icon to the right of the Custom Lead Page name to edit the page options.

Click the "Add New Page" link in the upper-right corner to add a new lead page.



 

Add Custom Lead Page

Enter a name for your new Lead Page.  You can easily create a new Lead Page by copying an existing Lead Page from the list box at the bottom of the page.  Click the "Add" button at the bottom of the page to add your new Lead Page.

You can view a list of custom lead pages from any other group by entering the desired group number in the text box below the list and clicking the "Reload" button.

Your newly created Lead Page will contain a copy of all the content from the copied Lead Page.

Edit Custom Lead Page

In your list of Custom Lead Pages, click the pencil  icon to the right of the Custom Lead Page name to edit the page options.

You can edit the Lead Page name and set the status to active when you have completed editing the Lead Page and it is ready to be used.  Select the sales campaign and the prospect type that will be automatically set for any new prospect that responds to this Lead Page.

You can edit the page options and content at the bottom of the page. 

Your Lead Page actually consists of three web pages.  The first one is the presentation that will be displayed to the visitor.  It contains the simple one-page presentation of the primary benefit that you are promoting to the target market.  You only have a few minutes to get their interest with your Lead Page, so keep it short and focused on the primary benefit.  

The second page is a Thank You page that is displayed to the user after they submit their information.

The third page is the default email template that is used when you email this Lead Page to someone.

 Click the pencil  icon to the right of the page name to edit the page options.

  

Edit Page Options

You can edit your page name, number, status and language for the current page.  

If the page is the presentation page, it will display a Custom Fields text box at the bottom of the page.  These custom fields correspond with the data capture form fields on your lead page and they tell the system how to process the user's input when they submit their information.  

Normally you do not need to modify these custom fields unless you need to capture different information from your new prospect.

Custom fields with the standard names of Company, LastName, FirstName, Title, Email, Phone, Phone2, Street, Unit, City, State, Zip,Country and Website will be copied into the corresponding fields on the new prospect's detail record.  All other custom fields will be stored in the prospect's description field.

The name of the custom field must match the name of the HTML input element.

Click the blue question mark  icon to learn more about the custom field definitions.

  

Edit Page Content

You can edit the content of each of the web pages that makeup your Lead Page from the page list at the bottom of the edit page.

On the right side of the list are several icons to assist you with editing the page content.  The preview  icon will display the selected page.  The download  icon will allow you to save the page to your personal computer for editing with your HTML webpage editor. 

You can use any HTML editor like Microsoft FrontPage, Microsoft Expression or Adobe Dream Weaver.  If you do not have an HTML webpage editor you can download a free and professional WYSIWYG HTML editor at  http://www.kompozer.net/download.php.

After you've completed your editing of the web page you can upload the page by clicking the upload  icon.  The uploaded file must have a *.htm extension.

To assist you with the editing of your web pages you can click the Stock Images icon above the page list.  This will display a list of images that are available for your use on your web pages.  Copy the URL included with each image to your HTML editor image source.  You may or may not have the Stock Images option.  

You may also upload your own custom images by clicking the Upload Files icon above the page list.  This will allow you to upload your own images to the server for their use in your web pages.  Copy the URL specified with the uploaded file to your HTML editor image source.

All resources in your web page must be accessed on the server including stock images, custom images, CSS style sheets, Flash movies, etc.



 


  

Edit Thank You and Email Pages

Thank you and email pages include an extra edit  icon in the page list. Click this "Edit Page Content" icon to edit your page content with a simple WYSIWYG (What-You-See-Is-What-You-Get) Editor to apply formatting similar to a word processor.  You can use the three editor toolbars to select the font style, color and size, format and align the text as well as many other functions.  
 

  

Replaceable Tags

You can insert tags that will be replaced with your's or the prospect's personal information.  The following replaceable tags are supported by the different page types.

The most useful replaceable tag is the {signature} tag.  It will insert the appropriate lead page signature from your list of defined signatures.

Presentation Page
{signature} your lead page signature
{name} your first and last name OR company name if company
{email} your email address
{phone} your day-time phone number
{image} your uploaded image
{id} your internal id number
{ref} your internal reference number
{p-firstname} prospect's first name
p-lastname} prospect's last name
{p-email} prospect's email address
{p-phone} prospect's day-time phone number
{p-id} prospect's internal id number


Email Page
{signature} your email signature
{m-firstname} your first name
{m-lastname} your last name
{m-email} your email address
{m-phone} your day-time phone number
{m-id} your internal id number
{p-firstname} prospect's first name
p-lastname} prospect's last name
{p-email} prospect's email address
{p-phone} prospect's day-time phone number
{p-id} prospect's internal id number
{link} system generated link to the Lead Page

The {link} tag in the email must not be modified.  It will be replaced by the system with the system generated link to the lead page with all the required parameters including which lead page, your id and the prospect's id if known.  


Thank You Page
{signature} your email signature
{m-id} your internal id number
{p-firstname} prospect's first name
p-lastname} prospect's last name
{p-email} prospect's email address
{p-phone} prospect's day-time phone number
{p-id} prospect's internal id number

  

Continue to Presentation

You can also send the Lead Page visitor directly to a Presentation after they submit their information by including the following link on your Thank You page.
newPP.asp?p=[x]&m={id}&r={p-id}

where [x] is the Presentation number (without the brackets) following the name in your presentation list.
