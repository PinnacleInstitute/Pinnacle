Make the Transition to Word 10
Step 1: AutoCorrect

This feature provides an easy mechanism for users to control and modify automatic behavior that takes place within Word. Users can undo an automatic correction, choose to not have that correction take place in the future, or access the AutoCorrect Options dialog box.
Step 2: Smart Tags

In previous versions, Word was smart enough to recognize URLs and e-mail names, and assign links associated with them. Users then could activate the items by clicking them. Word 10 extends this functionality by recognizing names, addresses, and dates. When an item is recognized, the user is provided with a Smart Tag that allows them to obtain additional information. Additional Smart Tags will be available on the Office Update Web site and can be extended by organizations and third parties to offer unique document solutions.
Step 3: Translate

Word 10 gives users the ability to translate words in their document (available languages are determined by language dictionaries that are installed) or to access a translation service on the Web. This service provides entire document translation as well as additional languages.
Step 4: Word Count

The Word Count toolbar allows users to easily update the word count in a document without having to go to the Word Count dialog box. Additional improvements in Word Count allow users more flexibility in whether headers and footers are counted in their document.

Start Word
Step 1: Start Word

Open Word XP if it is not already open. To do this, go to the Start menu and select Programs/Microsoft Word.
Step 2: New Document

When you first start Word 10, it automatically opens a new document. Go ahead and type a few lines of text, so you'll have something to work with for this tutorial.
Step 3: The Toolbar

The toolbar contains some useful buttons, and in the next few steps you'll be using the New, Open, Save, and Print buttons. Take a minute to find them on the toolbar. Depending on how your version of Word XP is set up, you may find your buttons move around the toolbars a bit. This is because Word keeps track of the buttons you use most and moves them to the left of the toolbar. However, if you hold your mouse over a button, a tool tip displaying its name will appear.
Step 4: Save

Save your file by clicking the Save button (or selecting Save from the File menu). The Save As dialog box appears. The drop-down box labeled "Save in" shows the folder in which Word is suggesting you save your file (usually the My Documents folder). The drop-down box labeled "File name" shows the name Word is suggesting you use to call your file- by default it shows the first few words you typed in the document. Click Save to save your file.

Start a New Document
Step 1: Start Word

Open Word XP if it is not already open. To do this, go to the Start menu and select Programs/Microsoft Word.
Step 2: Select from the Menu

When you first launch Word, a blank screen will appear. If you don’t see a blank white typing screen, choose File and select New from the menu bar to start a new document. The New dialog box will appear. Click on the General tab and double-click on the Blank document icon. A blank typing screen will appear.
Step 3: Look Around

Look around the screen to get your bearings. Pause the mouse for a few seconds over each button that appears on the top row of the screen or on the toolbar. A tip will appear showing what each button does.
Step 4: Start Typing

Begin typing. When you reach the right margin, the text will automatically wrap around to the next line. Press the enter key only when you have finished typing a paragraph of information, or at the end of short lines of address information.

Correct Your Document
Step 1: A Red Wavy Line

When you type any word that Word can't find in its dictionary. The word gets a red wavy line under it to tell you it may be misspelled (in this figure, for example, Word points out "ussing" as a possible misspelling).
Step 2: Display the Shortcut Menu

Right-click the word to open a shortcut menu displaying possible alternative spellings. To choose an alternative, click it. If your spelling is correct (for example, someone's last name), click Add; the word is added to Word's dictionary and won't be picked up by the Spelling Checker again.
Step 3: Ignore the Spelling

To leave the word spelled as is in the current document, click Ignore All from the short-cut menu. To add the correct spelling to your AutoCorrect list, click AutoCorrect.
Step 4: A Green Wavy Line

If the wavy underline is green, Word detects a possible grammatical error. Follow steps 2 and 3 to fix the error.

Enhance Your Work
Step 1: Click the Appropriate Button

Once you have entered your text, you will want to give emphasis to particular words by using the bold, italic or underline formatting features. Select the text you want to format, or choose the formatting commands before typing the text. To boldface text, click the Bold button on the Formatting toolbar. To italicize text, click the Italic button. To underline text, click the Underline button.
Step 2: Formatting Applied

Depending on which buttons you select, the formatting is immediately applied to your selected text.
Step 3: Open the Font Dialog Box

If you want to see what the formatting will look like before you apply it, open the Format menu and choose Font. This opens the Font dialog box with the Font tab displayed.
Step 4: Use the Font Dialog Box

In the Font style list, choose Bold, Italic, or Bold Italic to boldface or italicize your text. To underline text, click the down arrow on the Underline list box to display the list of choices, and then click the desired underline style.
Step 5: Preview Your Selections

In the Preview area at the bottom of the dialog box, you can preview how your choices will affect the text.
Step 6: Exit the Dialog Box

When you've made your selections, choose OK to close the dialog box and apply the changes to the selected text.

Undo Problems
Step 1: Undo

Click the Undo button to reverse your most recent action, such as typing a word or deleting something.
Step 2: Redo

Click the Redo button to restore the last action you reversed.
Step 3: Undo Drop Down Menu

Use the Undo drop down menu and then click the actions you want to reverse.
Step 4: Redo Drop Down Menu

Use the Redo drop down menu and then click the actions you want to restore.

Save Your Work
Step 1: Save for the First Time

Click the Save button on the toolbar. Click the pop-up menu that appears and select the folder where you want to save the file. Enter a name for the file and then click Save.
Step 2: Save a File With a New Name

Click the File menu and select Save As. Enter a new name in the Save dialog box. Click Save.
Step 3: Save a File in a New Place
Click the File menu and select Save As. Click the pop-up menu and then select the folder where you want to save the file. Click Save.
Step 4: Save a File in a New Format

Click the File menu and select Save As. Click the Save File As Type drop down list and select the file type you want. Click Save.

Back Up Your Work
Step 1: Be Safe

You may want additional copies of each of your documents. The Always create backup copy option tells Word to save a second copy of everything you do. Although having a backup copy is a safeguard, it does take up twice as much disk space because Word is keeping two copies of each document.
Step 2: Open Options

To start creating backup copies of documents choose Tools and select Options from the menu bar. The Options dialog box will appear.
Step 3: Choose Save Tab

Click on the Save tab and check the Always create backup copy option.
Step 4: Click OK

When you have finished making your selections, click the OK button. Now whenever you save a document, a backup copy will be created.

Spell Check Your Work
Step 1: Open Spelling & Grammar

Click at the beginning of the document and then click the Spelling and Grammar button on the toolbar. As it checks each sentence in the document, Word highlights misspelled words in the Not in Dictionary box or problematic sentences in the Sentence box and displays an appropriate alternative in the Suggestions box.
Step 2: Make a Substitution

To make a substitution, select any of the suggestions and then click change.
Step 3: Skip the Word

To skip a word or rule, click ignore or to skip every instance of the word or rule, click ignore all.
Step 4: Do it Yourself

If none of Word’s suggestions seem appropriate, click in the document and edit yourself. Click the Resume button to continue.

Preview and Print
Step 1: Select Print

Click the File menu and select Print. Click the Printer drop down list and select the printer you want.
Step 2: How Many?

Specify the pages you want to print and how many copies you would like.
Step 3: Options?

Click the Options button at the bottom of the dialog box to set other options.
Step 4: Print

When you’re satisfied with your selections, click OK.

Close Your Work
Step 1: Use the File Menu

Click the File menu and then select Close.
Step 2: Use the Close Box

Click the Close box on the title bar.
Step 3: Save and Close

Click the File menu and then select Save. Save the file and then close it.
Step 4: Exit

Click the File menu and select Exit.

Open an Existing Document
Step 1: From the Desktop

Double click the hard drive icon in which the file is stored. Next double click the folder in which the file is stored, finally double click a file name to start the program and open that file.
Step 2: Within a Program

Click the Open button on the Standard toolbar. Click the Select a Document pop-up menu and then select the drive where the file is located. Double click the file you want to open.
Step 3: From the File Menu

If the file you want to open is a file you’ve worked on recently, it’s probably still stored in the bottom of the File menu. While in Microsoft Office 2001, click the File menu; select the document you want from the list and release the mouse button.
Step 4: Using the Open Dialog Box

Click the Open button on the toolbar. Click Find File in the Open dialog box. Enter the name of the file you wish to find and click OK. Microsoft Office XP will find the file on your disk and open it.

Switch Between Open Documents
Step 1: Resize and Move a Window

Use the sizing boxes or the mouse pointer to resize and move the document window.
Step 2: Zoom Box

Click the Zoom box to make a window fill the entire screen. Click again to return it to its original size.
Step 3: Collapse/Expand

Click to hide a window leaving the title bar. Click it again to show the window.
Step 4: Mouse Pointer

Drag a window’s borders to resize it or drag a title bar to move it. Double click the title bar to collapse it, and double click again to expand it.

Work With Toolbars
Step 1: Display and Hide

Click the View menu and select Toolbars. Click on the name of the toolbar you want to show or hide.
Step 2: Move and Resize

To move a docked toolbar to a floating location double click or drag the gray bar on the left edge of the toolbar. To move a floating toolbar, click its title bar and drag it to a new location.
Step 3: Customize a Toolbar Part 1

Click the View menu and select Toolbars. Now click Customize. If the toolbar you want to customize is not displayed, click the Toolbars tab and then click the check box next to it. Now click the Commands tab.
Step 4: Customize a Toolbar Part 2

To add a button to a toolbar, click the type of button you want to add in the Categories box and then drag a button from the Commands box to the toolbar where you want it to appear. To move a button, drag it to a new location on any visible toolbar. To remove a button, just drag it off the toolbar. When you’re done, click Close.

Menu & Dialog Box Options
Step 1: Choose Menu Commands

A menu is a list of related commands. Click a menu name on the menu bar at the top of the program window.
Step 2: Use Control

You can also use the right mouse button to bring up menu commands. Click an object while holding down the right mouse button key. A menu of commands that pertains to that object will appear.
Step 3: Arrows?

If an arrow follows the menu command you want, point to it and open a submenu of related commands.
Step 4: Click

Click a menu command to choose that command or to open a dialog box.
