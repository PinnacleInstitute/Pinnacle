EXCHANGE RATE POLICIES

 

  Especially after WWII, the international trade in good and services has increased steadily. The expansion of the international trade brought the dependency of the countries to each other. When we look at U.S, -in terms of  trade-, many Goods and services, including basic life supplies  come from East Asian ( mostly China) also  Most of the U.S products had traveled around world and been the inevitable goods of the all people around the world.

 

  On the other hand, international trade may cause inappropriate situations when we  checked goods  and services sold to other countries. The economist mentioned about a n equivalence in international currencies during the trade.

This Equivalence is called Exchange rates.

In other words, Exchange rate between two countries is the price at which residents of the country trade with each other.  

For example in   finance part of newspapers we see the  notes as 1 $= 120 yen

Or 1$= 0.9 Euro. This equalizations are called exchange rates.

 Generally economists distinguish exchange rates as nominal exchange rates and real exchange rates.

              Nominal Exchange rate:

Is the relative price of the currencies of two countries. Like the previous example

1$=120 Yen

We figure out that the exchange rate between U.S dollar and Japan yen is 120 Yen/Dollar

 

This is the nominal     exchange rate.

For example

If a Japanese investor wants to buy 1 $ she/he will give  120 yen in order to buy  1$.

 

The nominal exchange rates are generally used in financial markets

On the other hand, unlike nominal exchange rates, Real Exchange rate is the relative price of currencies. This rate also explains the rate at which countries can trade goods and services of one country for the goods and services of other one.

 

 For example

Let say 1 American Car ( Ford Taurus,) costs 25000 $ for a single consumer and 1 German Car ( BMW ) costs 10000 Euro for  a consumer. If we think there are not tariffs  for exports and imports and transportation cost are negligible

Then 1 American Car is cost  25000 $*0.8Euro/Dollar=20.000 Euro

 On the other hand 1 german car  is 10000 Euro

We can compare and say that 1 American car is  twice expensive as 12 German car when we equalize the

Real Exchange rate= 2*German Car /American Car

This ratio gives us exchange rate.

Also

This is the real Exchange rate

The general Formula for  real exchange rate is

R=E*P/P×

E stands for the nominal Exchange rate

P stands for the price price of the good in demoestic country

P× stands for the price level of the good and services in foreign country

What Factors can effect  the exchange rates?

There are several factors effecting Exchange rates

  • The Effect of the increase in Output

 When the domestic output of a country increases, holding other factors constant, What happens to exchange rates?

When domestic income increases, consumers will spend more on Goods and services which will increase the demand for foreign currency . People have willing to exchange their money with foreign currency and  so that domestic currency supply will increase

So that the number of Dollars traded in the market will increase and the exchange rate will fall.

 

 

 Other factors are

  • The change in Foreign Income

As Higher foreign output raises, demand for exports ( as well as dollar) increase this will increasse the demand for domestic currency and will increase the Exchange rates

  • World Demand for Domestic goods

Higher world demand for domestic goods, means high demand for Dollar and increase demand for dollar and increase the Exchange rates.

  • Domestic interest rates

As the domestic interest rates increase, the return of dollar investment will increase so that demand for dollar increases. This will lead to an increase in Exchange rates.
