Importing Clips into the Clip Gallery

The Import Clips button in the Clip Gallery window allows you to bring graphic files from your computer into the Clip Gallery. These images will then be available through the Clip Gallery in any Office program.

Read through the steps: . If you would like to complete these steps in an actual Office file, create a new Office file and then proceed through the steps.

Practice the Skill
  1. From within an Office 2000 program, click >Insert>Picture>ClipArt to open the Clip Gallery.
    The Clip Gallery opens.

  2. At the top of the Clip Gallery window, click Import Clips.
    The Add clip to Clip Gallery dialog box opens.

  3. Navigate to the location of the file containing the picture that you want to import. Select the picture and choose one of the options under Clip import option . Usually, the default, Copy into Clip Gallery is the best.Then click Import.
    The picture is imported into the Clip Gallery and the Clip Properties dialog box opens.

  4. In the text box on the Description tab, enter a description of the clip, if desired. Select the Categories tab and choose which categories you want the clip to be placed in by checking the boxes beside them. Finally, select the Keywords tab and enter additional keywords for the clip so that it will appear when keyword searches are performed.Click OK when you have finished making your choices.
    The desired description, categories, and keywords for the imported clip are entered. The imported clip appears in a Clip Gallery window of its own.

  5. To verify that it has been placed in the appropriate category, click the Back button at the top of the Clip Gallery window, and then click on one of the categories which you chose in step 4. Look for the clip you just imported.
    The image you imported appears in one of the categories you chose.

Downloading Clips from the Microsoft Web Site

The Clips Online button in the Clip Gallery window allows you to add to the Office Clip Gallery files from Microsoft's Clip Gallery Live on the Web. You may download clip art images as well as animated graphics and sounds. This process works smoothly if Internet Explorer is your default Web browser. If Netscape Navigator is your default browser, the download process is not so smooth. Two modifications to the download process are suggested at the end of the step-by-step instructions below; either of them will allow you to use the download feature when Netscape Navigator is your default browser.


The Clips Online button for downloading from Clip Gallery Live

Read through the steps: . If you would like to complete these steps in an actual Office file, create a new Office file and then proceed through the steps.

Practice the Skill
  1. From within any MS Office 2000 program, click Insert>Picture>Clipart to open the Clip Gallery.
    The Clip Gallery opens.

  2. Click Clips Online at the top of the window.
    Your Web browser opens to Microsoft’s Clip Gallery Live.

  3. Browse the End User License Agreement and click to accept its terms.
    The Clip Gallery Live page opens.

  4. On the Clip Gallery Live page, use the search boxes to search the site for specific clips, or click on the links to browse the site.

  5. As you browse through the pages of clipart, click in the checkboxes to select clips you would like to download. If you want just one clip, click Download underneath it to download it immediately.

  6. After you have made your selections, click the Download link. Or you may click Selection Basket to view your selections and delete any that you do not want; then click Download. An information page opens; click Download Now to begin the downloading process.
    The selected clips are downloaded and the Clip Gallery opens.

  7. The clips that you selected will automatically be placed into the Downloaded Clips category in the Clip Gallery. Click on this category in the Clip Gallery window to open it.
    The Downloaded Clips category opens.

  8. To file your clips in appropriate places in the Clip Gallery, right-click on each and choose Properties. On the Description page you may write a description of the clip, if desired. On the Categories page, you may decide which categories you want the clip to be placed in by checking the box beside the category's name. Finally, on the Keywords page, you may enter additional keywords for the clip so that it will appear when keyword searches are performed. Click OK when you have finished making your choices.
  9. Another way to file clips into categories is to left-click on one of them, and choose the Add clip to Favorites or other category button from the graphic menu that appears. Use the dropdown box to choose a category for the selected clip and click Add. (To categorize several clips in the same category, hold down Ctrl as you select clips.)
    Clips are filed in the appropriate categories.

NOTE: When Netscape Navigator is your default browser and you attempt to download clips from within the Clip Gallery as described above, you are asked to save the clips to a file instead of the clips being automatically downloaded to the Clip Gallery. There are two ways to handle this situation:

  1. Save the clips to a file, renaming it if necessary so that you can remember which file contains the clips you downloaded. (The default names are not very helpful.) Once the clips have been downloaded, navigate to their location in either Windows Explorer or My Computer, and double-click on the file to open it.& The downloaded clips will appear in a window (actually the Downloaded Clips category).
    You may then categorize the clips as described in #7 and 8 above.

  2. An easier method for working around the difficulty when Netscape is your default browser is not to download from within the Office Clip Gallery. Instead, simply open Internet Explorer and to navigate to the Clip Gallery Live page (www.microsoft.com/clipgallerylive). Choose clips and download them as described above. The Clip Gallery will be automatically opened and the clips will appear in a window. Use methods described above to place the clips in the categories in which you want them to appear.
