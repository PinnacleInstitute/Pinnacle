Setting Goals for Success:

Do I need goal setting? How much success do you want? Many athletes achieve some

success without using formal goal setting, but virtually every great athlete who

consistently wins uses some form of goal setting. Based on our experience, the USOC

Sport Psychology program believes that using goal setting is as necessary as having a

coach.

How does goal getting help? Like have a coach, good goal setting gives you an edge in

three areas:

1) Direction – Goals tell you where you need to go and help guide you there.

2) Feedback – Goals tell you when you are making progress.

3) Support – Goals help to keep you going.

What happens if I don’t use goal setting? A lot of athletes say, "Hey, why should I do

goal setting when things are going so well?" If things are going well, you may not notice

the absence of goal setting right away. It’s when things start going poorly, however, that

the athlete who does good goal setting can correct things and avoid a slump. Remember,

good goal setting gives you direction, feedback, and support, three important tools when

you are trying to avoid a slump.

What is effective goal setting? Truth be told, we are what we do. Our behavior is the

key. A motivational speech is useless unless people behave differently because of it.

Reading self-help books, talking about making changes in your life, attending retreats are

wastes of time unless the end result is a change in behavior. Thus, effective goal setting

is a process that results in a behavioral change.

Athletes who use goal setting effectively find a number of behavioral changes including:

a) Training is more productive, with more done in less time.

b) Focused competition behavior, resulting in less nervousness at competitions

c) Athletes avoid plateaus, moving constantly forward in their training.

Effective goal setting becomes a habit. Like other behaviors that take some effort, goal

setting needs to become a habit, or it fades away. When goal setting become an

automatic part of your day, it will be most helpful to you.

Effective goal setting pays more attention to the little goals than the big goals. "If

you want to climb a mountain you must begin by taking a single step." Setting big goals

is easy. Developing a plan to reach those goals is hard. Effective goal setting begins

when you get past the big goals and start to build yearly, monthly, weekly, and daily

goals that get you to where you want to go. It takes work, and it needs to be a continual

process, but it can work wonders in your life.
