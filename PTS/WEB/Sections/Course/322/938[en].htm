Formatting the Letter



Whenever you want to format something in Word, the general rule is "select, and then do".
For example, you may want to make a block of text bold. You must select the block of text first then make it bold.

You can select text by click and dragging with the mouse. As you drag the mouse over the text you want, it becomes highlighted. Release the mouse button to select the highlighted text.
To cancel a text selection, you can simply click once or press one of the arrow keys.

Position the pointer somewhere inside your document and select some text. To select, press the mouse button and hold it down. Without releasing the mouse button, drag the pointer to the end of the sentence and release the mouse button.

Click the mouse button to deselect it.

There are selecting short cuts which will be explained below.

Deleting Text



Once you have selected text, one of the things you can do with that text is delete it. You can delete text permanently, or you can remove it from your document and place it on the clipboard, for temporary storage.

To delete text permanently, select it and then hit the delete key.

To delete a text selection from a document and move it to the clipboard, select the text and then click on the Cut button () located in the Standard toolbar.

You can also choose Cut from the Edit menu to place a copy of selected text onto the clipboard.

Changing Typeface and Type Size



Word offers a wide variety of type styles (fonts) for you to use. You can use fonts for emphasis or to provide variation in your document.
The type size of a particular font is related to the printer. Printers measure type size in points. A point is 1/72 of an inch, so 72-pointt type is 1 inch high. Most books are printed using 9- to 11-point type size. Word is capable of producing type sizes from 4 point to 127 points in 1/2 increments. Whether that full range of sizes is actually available to you will depend on the font you are using and on the printer.

There are two ways to change the font and font size.

Word allows you to indent a paragraph from the left and right margins and to indent the first line independently on the left. You can also leave the the first line unindented and indent the subsequent lines in the paragraph, which is called a hanging indentation.

You can use the mouse to set indentations on Word's ruler.

You can "grab" each indent marker with the mouse pointer and slide the marker to the left or right to adjust the indentation. To "grab" a marker, position the pointer over it and then click and hold down the mouse button, the marker will move with the pointer.

Understanding Paragraph Formatting


Every time you format something in Word that information is stored. The formatting information for a paragraph is stored in the paragraph mark () at the end of the paragraph.
If you delete the paragraph mark, the formatting information is also deleted. The paragraph will take on the formatting information of the paragraph it is joined with.
On the other hand, if the insertion point is inside a paragraph and you press the Return key to begin a new paragraph, the new paragraph takes on the formatting of the previous one.

Since Word has this feature, you will need to get comfortable with copying existing paragraph formats.
For example, you can create an additional centered paragraph in your letter using the name and address paragraph. Let's try it.

Position the insertion point at the end of the text 'Supercorporation' in your document.

Tab Stops



A tab is a marker that you define to allow you to move quickly to a specific position in a document. Tabs are used to align text at a location measured from the left margin of a document.

You can see the tabs set every half-inch along the ruler, indicated by small dots at the bottom edge of the ruler.

You can put custom tabs on the ruler by clicking along the ruler's bottom edge under the measure ment marks. Four kinds of tabs can be set from the ruler. The four kind of tabs are located in the Tab Alignment box located on the Formatting toolbar.

Observe:


If you click on the Tab Alignment button you will notice that there are four different styles of tag stops: left, center, right, and decimal.

The Left tab will align text along the left edge of the tab stop.

The Center tab will center lines at the tab stop.

The Right tab will align text along the right edge of the tab stop.

The Decimal tab will align text or more commonly numbers on the decimal point under the tab stop.

Locate the Tab Alignment button and click on it to see the four style of tab stops mentioned above.
