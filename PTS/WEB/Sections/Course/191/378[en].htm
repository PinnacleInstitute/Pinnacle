Please sit down in a silent place with a notepad in which you can compile all materials that you are going to write through the course.

1) Body:

Please focus for a moment on your body.

Please find a situation where you were 100% satisfied with the performance of your body. It can be a situation during a competition, where you won the first prize, it can be a training that you are fully satisfied with and tell people around what fun it was. It can be any other situation that you evaluate as a kind of an example and would like to repeat many times.

When you decide what situation you want to focus on please write it down.

  • Note everything you can remember, all details
  • Feel how your body moved and describe it
  • feel how you felt and write it down in detail
  • Feel your emotions, remember the scenery colors, sounds, smells and pictures.

Put it all on paper.

2) Mind:

Focus for a moment on your mind.

Think of situation in which the greatness of your mind impressed even yourself.

What situation can it be?

  • Passing impossible exam,
  • getting a challenging job,
  • managing a tough project,
  • learning a foreign language.

Anything that impressed you will do. Again return in your mind back to that situation and describe it in detail. But focus only on yourself:

  • What was happening with you,
  • your thoughts, emotions, feelings.
  • All details of how your mind worked.

Put it on a paper.

3) Spirit:

The same process regarding the area of Spirit.

  • Think of the moment you felt spirited.
  • Think of situations where you could feel synchronicity of events happening almost miraculously.
  • Think of moments you felt close to Nature or close to God.
  • Describe what you did, what did you think about.
  • Describe your thoughts, feelings, emotions.
  • Describe colors, sounds, smells, etc.
  • Create a detailed picture.

Put it on a paper.

4) Finding WaKi:

Now is the time to find "Ideal WaKi". What is it?

It is a process of finding in the above, highly energizing, events common elements - the internal harmony which is being created during each successful situation.

Read through your notes on Body/Mind/Spirit situations that you consider to be higly successful. In each of the situation, there was a moment that reached your deep inner world. 

Please sit down in a quiet place 

  • First read through your notes and try to find common elements.
  • Write down all that you notice

Next comes the time for a dialog with your inner part.

  • Sit down comfortably and in your imagination go back to the events.
  • Ask your unconscious to show you the elements that you did not notice during your conscious work.
  • Recall as many details as possible and focus on your inside.
  • Note which part of you is resonating in those events, which part or what inside you is active during those events.
  • Please write immediately anything that will come to your mind. It doesn't need to be in clean, clear writing. Write as you feel.

The above instruction can be very vague, or frustrating for some beginners. Some may say, I don't have any situation, where I were 100% satisfied. Others will think of a nice situation, but will not be sure if they are right in what they do. Others will not understand what they are supposed to do. This is a natural process. Our unconscious does not like change. The unconscious loves situations IT is used to.

Therefore, not understanding a task, not being sure if they way we do things is right is a standard reaction.

If you do not understand what to do, first of all do not blame yourself. Simply say to yourself:

"Dear unconscious! Thank you for protecting me and keeping me on the safe side. Grateful as I am, I want to inform you that today is the starting day of our search for even greater safety. I need your help to develop the kind of safety where both of us: I and You will enjoy total Peace of mind. Please help me to discover the moments where both of us enjoyed the ultimate safety and Peace of mind. Please assist me in making sure that the changes we are about to perform are for the greatest benefit for You and Me and for the greatest good of all that will be involved in any way during the process."

For those who do not know what to do, let's have an example.

1) Body:

For me an ideal situation where my body functioned perfectly was a fight simulation for an exam in a Japanese martial art Shorinji Kenpo.

I was very excited to perform in front of a big audience, but at the same time I was incredibly focused on a spot within my belly. A slight tension applied to that spot allowed me to feel confident, fast and strong. In a moment the audience disapeared into the background. I still was aware of them, in the sense of feeling the distance to them, but my focus was pointed to my partner. I could feel a strong electrical current running through my body. My breathing became very regular and rather slow. I could sense the slightest movement of my partner, I could enjoy the stilness within my body. All sounds disapeared into the background and there was a little tensed silence.

We started our performance.

Our moves, even though quite fast, seemed to be in slow motion. Therefore, there was no problem with timing, because each move was so clearly visible that even when my partner made a mistake, I had no problem with reacting in a correct way and keeping the flow of our performance.

There was an additional aspect that I didn't notice during the presentation. I learned about it later on and understood it years later. In a special moment where my partner caught my sleeve, I countered, made a slight "atemi" that is a distracting strike to the ribs and then a spectacular throw in the air.

What I din't realized at that time, was the fact that even though I didn't apply much force to the strike into my partner's ribs, I was intensly focused. That focus added energy to my strike and broke my partner's rib. At first I blamed myself for not being carefull enough. Today I understand what I didn't know at that time. I didn't know that there are two different kinds of strike: one with the body and another with the mind. At that time unaware of the consequences, along with a hand stroke, I applied a strong stroke with my focused mind. When we finished, we got a big applauze and I could feel a strong satisfaction and pride to be able to perform like this. This was reinforced by winning the first prize in competition.

Now in the story above where can we find an "ideal WaKi"?

"Wa" means harmony, so for me the ideal "Wa" is:

    • a feeling of a harmony with the spot in my belly that supported confidence, speed and strenght.
    • a feeling of harmony with the audience
    • a feeling of intense harmony with the partner
    • a strong focus on body movement
    • effortless and smooth movements, flowing each after another
    • a union of body and mind, where mind followed a stroke
    • a feeling of calmness during the whole very fast and hard performancea sense of a slow breathing in harmony with body movements
    • a sense of accomplishment and joy
    • a sense of pride towards my body and myself for giving an ultimate performance.

The ideal "Ki" is:

  • the feeling of strong electrical current flowing through the body, strenghtening it and allowing for a special kind of performance.
  • feeling of strong response from the spot in the belly, and in a way, being able to almost consciously communicate with it.
  • a feeling of joy and fullfilment after performance, which was very energizing and supportive towards reaching new goals.
