和気道 WaKiDo

What is this? What does WaKiDo means?

In a Japanese-English Dictionary. You will find a word

和気 = Wa-Ki

which means:

  • a peaceful state of mind
  • a relaxed state of mind
  • a harmonious relationship
  • a warm, pleasant and peaceful weather

The philosophy of WaKiDo is focused around these principles with an addition of Japanese meaning of each character used. If you have a Kanji (Japanese Characters) Dictionary, I suggest, you check the meaning of each character. You will see quite a number of meanings for each character. The same is in case of the word we want to check - Wa, Ki, Do. I will quote my Meiji Shoin KanWa Dictionary. What do we get here?

和 Wa： 

 "Wa" in the dictionary is described as:

  1. やわらぐ yawaragu/yawarageru: to soften something, to calm down　(温和 onwa）, to become peaceful (平和 heiwa)
  2. なごむ nagomu:　to become good friends
  3. 応じる oujiru: in general this word would be translated as "to answer", but answering here means to "react" accordingly to "actions" that is taken towards me, something like in Aikido. But regarding the reaction there is a very interesting value that Wa brings. This is:
  4. 中和 chuwa: that is neutralizing a toxic substance or harmonizing a negative situation, relationship.

By the way, did you notice presence of word "Wa" in the above "KanWa" Dictionary? It is the same "Wa" as in WaKiDo. The "Kan" part in "KanWa" means: "Kanji", that is Japanese characters and "Wa"? 

"Wa" in the word "KanWa" points to Japanese language. 和・大和 "Wa" or "Yamato" here means Japan.

気 Ki：

Nowadays "Ki", or sometimes used from Chinese "Chi", has become a very popular word. Therefore, here I will focus more on the meaning WaKiDo is most interested in:

  1. Ki (心 kokoro）: mind (heart),
  2. Ki (意向 ikou）： a mind, an intention, a wish
  3. Ki （気質 kishitsu)： spirit, nature
  4. Ki (空気 kuuki）： air
  5. Ki (霊気 reiki）： spiritual energy

 

道 Do：

Martial Arts have made this word very popular. And the meaning of this word here comes straight from the martial arts as a Way , Art of Transformation.

    How did WaKiDo come to existence?

     

    WaKiDo developed from two elements:

    1. The natural inclination to be in a state of Peace, very much derived from the name which means: "to praise the Peace"
    2. The interest in traditional Japanese martial arts, especially in its restraint from fighting and in its healing. Both deal very much with 「気」 = KI, like in 「合気道」 = AIKIDO or 「気功」 = KIKO.

    One can ask how Peace and Martial Arts can go together since Martial Art is used to destroy an opponent not to create Peace?

    Actually, a real Master in Martial Arts is a person of PEACE. A Master is focused on her/his inner world, not on the outside conflicts. Such a Master is so skilful and psychically strong, that she/he will difuse a conflict situation before any agression could take place.

    In WaKiDo, a martial art is one of means to enter your Inner World and to assist you in developing harmonious and energizing Inner Peace. We use the philosophy of Martial Arts, and continue martial arts training, but do not require that our clients actively train a martial art. The base for WaKiDo exercise is a non-martial-art set of exercises derived from the Japanese "Hida Shiki Kyou Ken Jutsu" which we call "WaKi Kata". This exercise has a very energizing effect and helps to develop straight spine, a base for free breathing and a free flow of your energy throughout your body. We also cooperate with the "Foundation for the Culture of Movement" and its experimental theatre OCELOT (www.ocelot-foundation.com) in developing movement that increases Inner Peace.
