Der, die oder das? - You have to choose

German has 3 different definite articles.
Instead of only "the" for all the nouns you have the choice between
'der, die oder das'  

6.1  Each noun has it’s own gender

 
The definite article shows the gender:

Masculine =  Der
Feminine  =  Die
Neuter       =  Das   

der Mann, der Kellner, der Kaffee
die Frau, die Kellnerin, die Milch
das Kind, das Restaurant, das Wasser

                              

6.2  Ok - So what helps me to find the correct article?

In general it's the best to learn new words always with the article.
Not everything follows specific rules in this matter, but 2 questions are very helpful:

  • What / Whom does the noun describe?
  • Which ending does the noun have?

6.4  And the plural... is always 'die'

singular plural
der Mann die Männer
die Frau die Frau
das Auto die Autos

Isn't that nice?
At least with the plural we don't have to worry about which of the three articles we should use.


6.5  Does German also have  indefinite articles?

The answer is Yes.

Definite Article Indefinite Article
der Mann ein Mann
das Auto ein  Auto
die Frau eine  Frau

So, the definite articles 'der + das' become to 'ein'.
Only female nouns have a different indefinite article - 'eine'.
