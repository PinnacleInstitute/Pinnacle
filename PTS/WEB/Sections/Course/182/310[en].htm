 8.2   Let's ask for the bill

             All right. All is eaten up and nobody is thirsty anymore and anyway you are ready to go home.
             Now it’s time to call the waiter and ask him for the bill. 

 
You Waitor ( Kellner )

Hallo... Wir wollen zahlen, bitte.

Gut. Hat es geschmeckt ?
(Did you like it?)
Ja, es war (sehr) gut!
Danke.

Zusammen oder getrennt?
(together or separately)
 Zusammen! / Getrennt!
Gut, also, Sie hatten ... ?
(so... you had?)
[ you tell him what you had ]
Gut.
Das macht .... (z. B. 22 ) Euro.
(This makes .... )
[ you give him the money + 10% tip ]
Danke. Auf Wiedersehen.
Auf Wiedersehen.


8.3   What about tipping?

Although it may somewhere mention in the menu, that the service charge is included in the price, it’s usual to tip with about 10% additionally to the amount.
Not to give a tip at all, might be considered as impolite or that you haven’t been satisfied with his / her service.

There are basically three "tipping techniques":

Lets say the required amount is 15.70 Euro

1. You give him 17 Euro and say: "Stimmt so." – (It’s ok)

2. You give him a 20 – Euro bill and say: " 17 " ( really, you don’t have to say more)

3. If you don’t say anything he will give you the change back.
    Then you give him 1 or 2 or whatsoever Euro as tip, saying: "Bitte"

8.5   How are Euro amounts expressed? 

Now, when the waiter tells you how much the bill is, he might say:

"Das macht 15 Euro 30."     This is the amount of 15.30 Euro.

First the full number is mentioned and then after "Euro" the cent amount.
