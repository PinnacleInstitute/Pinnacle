3.2 They all end with  -en ?

  • Well, the infinitive of German verbs ends usually with the ending  -en
  • This is the form of verbs you will find in your German dictionary
  • It's good to know this infinitive form first

 

3.3 Some important personal pronouns

However, the endings of the verb change according to the person doing the action. Sounds complicate?
Well, first of all make yourself familiar with these  really good words:

German Englisch
Ich I
Du You
Er He
Sie She
Es It
Wir We
Ihr You (plural)
Sie They
Sie You (polite)

Did you notice? German uses three different 'Sie'.
One is for one female person, one is for a group of people and the third is used to approach a stranger or a person of respect politely. So, you wouldn't you use 'Du' in this case.


 

3.4  Skip the ending and replace it

Say, you found the verb  - "kommen", to come - in your dictionary.
Now how would express  " I come" ?


First of all: Take the infinitive kommen and skip the ending  -en

 
Then: Add the ending for 'Ich' to the rest of the verb


 


 

3.5  And here an overview over the different endings of regular verbs

Any regular verb is conjugated according to this pattern:

Ich komme +e
Du kommst +st
Er, Sie, Es kommt +t
Wir kommen
Ihr kommt +t
Sie (plural) kommen
Sie (polite) kommen

Positive: For 'Wir' and the plural + polite 'Sie' there are no changes!

Of course, irregular verbs have different endings, not following this pattern - as their name already indicates.
