11.3  Öffnungszeiten - Opening times – could be a trap :-)

German opening times are regulated by law.
Stores may open at 8.00 / 9.00 a.m. ,and they close by 6:00 p.m. 
Bigger supermarkets close at 8.00 p.m.
Some smaller stores may close between noon and 2:00 p.m. for lunch. 

With German banks you could get the impression they are more closed than opened.
Anyway, don’t count on banks being open later than 4:00 pm.

On Saturdays smaller stores close at 13:00 or 14:00.
Commendably, just recently bigger stores have started staying open till 8:00.

And, surprise surprise: Many bakeries are open for 3 hours on Sunday morning.

 

Many shops have their own "individual" opening times. It may be "wise" to stop by and ask for the "Öffnungszeiten". Otherwise you could stand in front of closed doors ...

You                                                                          Shop assistant

Wann schließen Sie?
[When do you close?]

 (or)

Wann öffnen Sie?
[When do you open?]
 

Haben Sie mittags geöffnet?
[Are you opened at noon time?]
 

Wann schließen Sie samstags?
[When do you close on Saturday?]

Wir öffnen / schließen um ........... Uhr.

Vielen Dank. Tschüß.

 

11.4   Just looking...

Sometimes you just want to check out the merchandise on your own without anybody's  assistance. 

However, a friendly store assistant may "coincidentally" stand next to you and ask:

"Suchen Sie etwas bestimmtes?"       -  "Kann ich Ihnen behilflich sein?"
   Are you looking for something in particular?       Can I help you?  

  A straight  NEIN !!  would leave no doubt what you mean, but it wouldn’t be polite.

NEIN!!!!

To be polite you could express it like that:

You, der Kunde                                         Shop assistant

Kann ich Ihnen behilflich sein?

Danke.

Ich möchte mich nur umsehen.

Aber natürlich.
Rufen Sie mich, wenn Sie eine Frage haben.

11.5 Finding things in the store

But maybe you know exactly what you want and that this shop has it. And maybe you don’t have much time either. The easiest way to avoid "wandering" in the store, is to ask the shop assistant where you find the

Let’s say you are at a big department store and interested in nothing else but shoes...

Du, als Kunde                                              Shop assistant - Verkäufer

Entschuldigung.

Wo finde ich Schuhe, bitte?

Ja. Schuhe sind oben in Etage 3.

Danke.

11.7  You may find the item - but there are some more questions open:

You Shop assistant - Verkäufer
Was kostet das?
[ What does it cost? ]
Das da? - Das kostet 15 Euro.
ooops !! - 15 Euro – too expensive !
Haben Sie das billiger?
(Do you have something cheaper?)
Ja, wir hätten da .... [and then they show something else]
Haben Sie das - in Grün?

    • etwas kleiner?
    • etwas größer?

 Did you notice the –er after the adjectives? - Why that?

You add –er to the adjective to use it for comparisons.

  Examples:     Das Buch ist billig. Ist das Buch billig-er ? (cheaper)

                      Mein Auto ist schnell. Unser Ferrari ist schnell-er ! (faster)

                      Mein Haus ist groß. Mein Haus ist größ-er !  (bigger)
