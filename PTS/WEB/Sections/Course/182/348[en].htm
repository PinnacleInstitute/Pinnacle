Das Datum in Deutsch 
14.1    How to express dates in German:

Good, let’s say,   we have    August 16th, 2003   
This date we transform into date the German way 

  1. Germans mention first the day, and then the month:

    not: "August 16th"     rather:     "16. August"

  2. The day has to expressed as ‘ordinal number’ :

not: " Am sechzehn August "    - Of course, this matters especially in spoken German

How do you build ordinal numbers in German?

>>>>> You simply add – te to all numbers from 1-19 <<<<< 

English German
First Erste not:  Ein-ste
Second Zweite
Third Dritte not:  Drei-te
Fourth Vierte
Fifth Fünfte
Sixth Sechste
Seventh Siebte not:  Sieben-te
Eighth Achte
Ninth Neunte
Tenth Zehnte
Eleventh Elfte
Twelve Zwölfte
Thirteenth Dreizehnte
Fourteenth Vierzehnte
Fifteenth Fünfzehnte
Sixteenth Sechszehnte
Seventeeth Siebzehnte
Eighteenth Achtzehnte
Nineteenth Neunzehnte

To all the rest (ending with -ig ) you add - ste  

Twentieth:  Zwanzig – ste

Thirtieth:    Dreißig – ste

Fortieth:     Vierzig - ste

so, August 16th would be then: 
 

spoken:    der sechzehn-te August
written:    16. August

14.3  And sometimes you need the year:

  • The years 2000 and above are easy to express:

    1000  =   eintausend

2000  =   zweitausend

          2004  = "2 + Tausend und Drei"

    • Years below 2000 are expressed a bit differently:

1900 = neuzehnhundert

1800 = achtzehnhundert

1990  = "19 + Hundert neunzig"
