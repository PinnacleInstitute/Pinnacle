DIET SECRET #1:
Dieting will NOT eliminate fat.
           Your body can’t discriminate between intentional calorie deprivation (as in a diet), and starvation.  When you dramatically reduce your caloric intake, your body shifts into a protective mode by slowing your metabolism down and holding onto fat (an important energy source) and burning muscle instead.  In the beginning of a diet you WILL lose weight by dramatically cutting calories.  But it won’t be fat loss, it will be water weight and lean muscle tissue – the exact OPPOSITE of what you want to get rid of.
 
Not only will harsh diets slow your metabolism down to a crawl, causing your initial weight loss to come to a gradual halt, they will also inevitably bring about a “rebound” effect.  This rebound will make you even fatter than you were before starting the diet.  When you rebound, not only do you generally put on more weight than you actually lost with the diet, your percentage of body fat generally increases because your body cannibalized muscle tissue as an energy source during the dieting process.  Thus the “yo-yo” effect that almost all dieters experience.
 
To permanently lose the fat stores in your body, you’ve got to burn more calories and increase your metabolic rate (the rate at which your body burns fuel throughout the day – even when you’re NOT exercising) with a precise exercise routine and proper nutrient ratio adaptations (that means eating the right stuff at regular intervals).  Even if you don’t exercise (but I recommend you do), just eating 5-6 small, high quality meals each day (and by a meal, I mean anything from a nutritious snack to a sit-down dinner) will substantially increase your metabolism – and you’ll burn more calories!

My Meal Plan's Recipes


 

Oatmeal

Put 1 package of dried cherries in 4 cups of water and bring to a boil.  Add 3 cups of regular (not quick) oatmeal and lower to simmer for 5 minutes.  Meanwhile blend 8 oz Vanilla Silk, 1 cup of Splenda and 3 scoops of whey protein powder until smooth.  Add to oatmeal when 5 minutes has elapsed.   Add 1/4 cup chopped walnuts.  Stir until well-blended.  Makes 6 servings.

 

Ginger Pan-Grilled Turkey

2 lbs. ground turkey breast

Ground ginger

3T Hoison Sauce

Pam Spray

 

Spray the bottom of a large pan with Pam and heat to medium-high.  Place turkey into the pan. 

Thoroughly coat the turkey with ground ginger, and add the Hoison Sauce.  Mix.  Pan grill until

the turkey glazes over and is well done.  Makes 8 3.5 oz servings.

 

Baked Sweet Potatoes

Put 4 large sweet potatoes in a Pam-sprayed baking pan. Do not poke.  Cover with tinfoil.  Bake at 350 for 2 1/2 hours or until dark syrup runs from the potatoes.  Split in each in 2. Makes 8 servings.

 

Pan-Grilled White Fish and Salsa

Put 32 oz of your favorite white fish (Roughy, Tilapia, etc) in a Pam-sprayed pan.  At about 1/2 way done, add 32 oz of salsa.  Pan grill until fish is white and flaky.  Makes 8 servings.

 

Sherry-Glazed Salmon

Combine 1/4 cup dry sherry, 2T brown sugar, 1T low-sodium soy sauce and 1/8t five-spice powder (Asian).  Bring to a boil, stirring occasionally.  Cook until reduced to 3T (about 5 mins).  Remove from heat; stir in 1t cider vinegar. Arrange 24 oz salmon on a broiler pan coated with Pam.  Brush fish with 1/2 of sherry mixture.  Bake at 375 for 5 mins, brush fish with remaining sherry and bake an additional 5 mins or until done.  Makes 4 servings.

 

Spiced Beat Risotto

Heat 2t olive oil in Dutch oven over med-high heat.  Add 1 cup chopped onion, sauté' 3 mins.  Add 1t minced ginger and 2t finely chopped rosemary, sauté' 1 minute.  Add 1/2 cup dry white wine, cook 3 mins or until liquid is nearly absorbed, stirring constantly.  Add 3 cups finely chopped beets, 1/2 cup water, 1/4 t fine sea salt, and 1 14.5 oz can vegetable broth, bring to a boil.  Cover, reduce heat, and simmer 20 minutes or until beets are tender, stirring occasionally.  Stir in 6 cups finely sliced Swiss Chard and cook for 5 mins.  Add 1/2 cup feta cheese, stirring until blended.  Sprinkle each serving with 1T chopped walnuts.  Makes 4 servings.

 

Asian Chicken and Cabbage

Combine 2T Hoison sauce, 1t bottled minced garlic, 1t bottled ground fresh ginger; spread evenly over 16 oz chicken breasts.  Place chicken on a broiler pan coated with Pam and broil 6 mins on each side or until done.  Meanwhile, add 1t vegetable oil to a Dutch Oven.  Sauté' 1/2 cup chopped onion.  Add 4 cups packaged coleslaw, 1t dark sesame oil, 1/2 t salt and 1/2 t black pepper.  Stir fry 5 mins, remove from heat.  Makes 4 servings.

 

Mixed Chilled Veggies

Put a large bag of mixed broccoli and cauliflower crowns in a large plastic bowl. Add 1/4 inch of water to the bowl.  Put in the microwave on high for 6 mins.  Remove.  Add a bunch of diced celery, 1 chopped red pepper, 1 small bottle of sliced green olives, 1/2 small jar of capers, 1 large sliced cucumber, and 2 bunches of diced green onions.  Mix 1 1/2 T sherry vinegar or red wine vinegar, 2t Dijon mustard, 1t extra-virgin olive oil, 1/4 t freshly ground black pepper, 1/8 t salt and 1 chopped garlic clove.  Drizzle over veggies.  Mix well.  Makes a lot!

 

Mashed Sweet Potatoes

Put 4 large sweet potatoes in a Pam-sprayed baking pan. Do not poke.  Cover with tinfoil.  Bake at 350 for 2 1/2 hours or until dark syrup runs from the potatoes.  Put in a large bowl and mash.  Leave skins on.  Add 3 scoops vanilla protein powder, Splenda to taste and 1/4 cup chopped walnuts.  Makes 8 servings.

 

Grilled Chilled Chicken Salad

2 lbs. Chicken Breast, Pan Grilled In Pam Spray

20 Red Grapes, Halved

1 Small Package Frozen Peas

1-2 T Fat-Free Mayo

Dill and Celery Seed Spices to Taste (~1t each)

 

Mix all together in a large mixing bowl.  Makes 7 servings.

 

Broiled Salmon with Sweet-and-Sour Cucumbers

Combine 2T Dijon mustard, 1T brown sugar and 1/2 t salt.  Brush over fillets.  Place fillets, skin sides down, on a broiler rack coated with cooking spray; broil 8 mins or until fish flakes easily when tested with a fork.

 

Place 4 cups thinly sliced cucumbers, packets of Splenda, 1/2t salt, 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar and 1t dried dill in a large bowl.  Cover and chill 2 hours.  Serve with salmon.  Makes 8 servings.

 

Pan-Seared Chicken and Artichokes

Drain 1 6-oz jar of marinated artichoke hearts, reserving marinade.  Sprinkle 1 1/2 pounds of chicken with salt and pepper; coat with cooking spray.  Heat a large nonstick skillet over med-high heat.  Add chicken; saute' 3 mins on each side.  Add 1/2 cup sliced green onions and 1 minced garlic clove; sauté' 1 minute.  Stir in reserved artichoke marinade and 1/2 cup dry white wine, scraping pan to loosen browned bits.  Bring to a boil, reduce heat, and stir in artichokes, 1T chopped pitted kalamata olives and 1 14 1/2 oz can of drained diced tomatoes.  Cook 2 mins or until thoroughly heated.  Sprinkle with 2T grated parmesan cheese.

 

Pancakes

Mix 8 egg whites and 1 scoop (32g) of vanilla protein powder.  Remove 50% of clumps.  Pour into a Pam-sprayed pan heated to medium.  Cook until most bubbles on the top of the pancake or popped, or until the underside is golden brown.  Lift pancake and spray underside with spray-butter before flipping.  Makes 2 pancakes=1 serving.

Warm Shredded Chicken Salad With Mango Chile Salsa

Combine 1/4 c soy sauce and 1T cornstarch in a medium bowl.  Stir until the cornstarch is dissolved, then stir in 1T sesame oil, 1T lemon juice and 1T grated ginger. Toss into a large baggie with 4 pounds of chicken strips.  Marinate for 20-30 mins. Combine 2 sliced mangos, 2 green onions, 1 minced Serrano pepper, 1 cup chopped cilantro, 3T lime juice and 1/4 t salt.  Let sit for 20-30 mins.  Coat a large skillet with Pam and set over med-high flame.  Add the chicken strips in a single layer and cook for 3-4 mins on each side until browned and cooked through.  Place chicken over beds of mango salsa.  Serves 4.

 

Tuna Salad

3 6-oz cans of white albacore tuna in water

20 Red Grapes, Halved

1 Small Package Frozen Peas

1-2 T Fat-Free Mayo

Dill and Celery Seed Spices to Taste (~1t each)

 

Mix all together in a large mixing bowl.  Makes 7 servings.

 

Herbed Turkey Breast

Prepare 2 pound turkey breast for cooking.  Pull up skin and rub 1T each of thyme, rosemary, sage, garlic powder, and pepper under skin.  Place in a broiling bag (this will make it extra moist!) and bake for ~20 minutes per pound or until meat thermometer registers 180 degrees.

 

Almond Green Beans

It's easiest to buy them already combined in frozen packages.

 

Stir Fried Tropical Pork Tenderloin

Drain 1 20 oz can of chunk pineapple in juice, reserving the juice. Combine juice, 2T reduced-sodium soy sauce, 1T cornstarch and 1t cayenne pepper.  Set aside.  Heat 1t vegetable oil in large skillet over high heat.  Sir-fry 2 cups fresh broccoli florets 1-2 mins.  Add 1 pound of half-inch chunks of pork tenderloin, stir-fry 5-6 mins or until pork is just done. Push mixture to edges of skillet.  Stir juice mixture to blend well and pour into center.  Cook and stir until thickened and bubbly.  Add pineapple and cook for 1-2 mins until heated.  Serve on a bed of lettuce, garnished with slivered almonds.  Makes 4 servings.

 

Salad Dressing (no sugar, no fat, min calories)

Mix balsamic vinegar, Dijon-style mustard, dried basil and Splenda to taste.  Drizzle over salad.

 

Egg Pie

Mix 2 1/2 dozen egg whites, reserving and including 3-4 of the yolks, with 1T dried sweet basil and 1 package of regular crumbled feta cheese.  Put in 2 glass pie baking dishes and bake for 45 minutes at 350 degrees.  Makes 6 servings.

 

Kickin' Chicken

Combine 1T Mrs. Dash Spicy, 1T Cajun Spice, 1t paprika in a large baggie.  Add 1 pound chopped chicken and toss to coat.  Put in large Pam-sprayed preheated skillet and cook until just done.

 

Spicy Swiss Chard

Heat 2t olive oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat.  Add 2 pounds chopped chard.  Sauté' 1 min or until slightly wilted.  Sir in 1/2t crushed red pepper and 3 minced garlic cloves.  Cover and cook 4 mins or until tender, stirring occasionally. Uncover and cook 3 more mins or until liquid evaporates.  Stir in 1T fresh lemon juice.  Makes 6 servings.  Note the chard will shrink up a lot, like spinach does.

Pan-Grilled Chicken and Salsa

Put 2 pounds of chopped chicken breast in a Pam-sprayed pan.  At about 1/2 way done, add 32 oz of salsa.  Pan grill until chicken is white and juicy.  Makes 7 servings.

 

Mandarin Orange Grilled Chicken Pizza

Put 1 whole wheat 10 inch tortilla in a toaster oven and bake for 5 minutes at 350 degrees or until cracker hard.  Remove from oven and layer 1/2 grilled chicken breast, 10 pieces of mandarin orange, 1/4 chopped red pepper, 1T sliced black olives and 1/2T feta cheese.  Put back into toaster oven for 2-5 mins at 350 degrees or until warmed.  Makes 1 serving.

 

BONUS Recipe!

Blend 4 oz Vanilla Silk and 4 oz water in a blender.  Add 1T almonds and pulse to chop.  Add 1 packet Splenda, 1 scoop chocolate whey powder, 1T Hershey's cocoa and 1T shredded coconut.  Blend until smooth.  You've just made an Almond Joy shake!  Enjoy!
