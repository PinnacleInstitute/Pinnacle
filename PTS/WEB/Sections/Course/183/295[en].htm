PRESENTATION OF SKILLS AND ACCOMPLISHMENTS

 

This lesson will focus on the development of your self-marketing brochure which effectively communicates your experiences, skills and accomplishments. The resume needs to provide the reader with an image of the individual’s career history that will create enough interest to open doors that may lead to opportunities. Most marketing experts will stress the importance of product packaging and the product brochure must capture and effectively present the fundamental essence the product being sold.

 

Too often the resume becomes a dry lengthy product description rather than a lively brochure. The challenge is to clearly link your product to the target markets identified. Like most writing tasks the goal is to settle on the desired format and efficiently organize the details included in the product description and marketing plan. Strive to write the draft without concern for style and polished presentation. Then utilize editing feedback to rewrite the resume to complete at least three to four additional drafts.

 

This critical effort with provide: a presentation of organized information so that skills can be linked to concrete achievements, an interesting career overview that stimulates further inquiry and a summary document that others can utilize in referral discussions.

 

The marketing brochure is designed to create interest in you as a “product” but many times reviewers may use information highlighted to reduce the pool of candidates. The critical information should include: accomplishments that demonstrate knowledge and skills, positions held with titles and time frame, scope of responsibilities, special talents. Also, ensure that the information can be easily assimilated, be concise, use sound writing techniques, present with language that creates links to target markets, stress action verbs and get help editing the finished document.

 

RESUME FORMAT

 

The chronological format is preferred which includes contact information, review of company experience that begins with most recent position and works back through previously held positions, a brief statement of responsibilities held in each position, significant accomplishments during time spent in each position, formal education and other special skills or associations. It is critical that special emphasis be focused on key accomplishments rather than extensive details of responsibilities.

RESUME REVIEW CHECKLIST

 

Career Summary:

 

Has the summary clearly and very briefly summarized past experience?

 

Does it highlight functional focus, special skills, personal expertise in a way that portrays unique capabilities?

 

Does is create interest to read further and act as a brief promotion?

 

Position Description:

 

Do the responsibilities link to career summary and stress top priorities of the position?

 

Is the presentation brief, clear and concise and use general industry terminology?

 

Accomplishments:

 

Are each started with a strong action verb?

 

Are the actions and results clearly stated with appropriate measurements?

 

Can you discuss further details for each accomplishment statement?

 

Education/Special Skills:

 

Is all formal education listed with date of degree or certification?

 

Do special skills and professional affiliations further “round out” capabilities?

 

Visual Impact:

 

Is the length no more than 2-3 pages with enough eye catching information on the first page?
