DEMONSTRATING SKILL VALUE

 

You have identified your personal and career values and it is now time to reflect upon what you have accomplished during your career. Accomplishments are the end results in your career which have provided recognition and satisfaction. These accomplishments are applied skills which have resulted in success.

 

To promote yourself, you need to convince the decision maker/s that you match the requirements for the opportunity. The candidate is selected based on what they can accomplish which may include solving problems, completing a project, creating new methods, etc. For this reason, you must link skills to accomplishments.

 

Making general statements without giving examples of results will weaken your chances for selection. Each element of your presentation needs to consistently reinforce the strength of accomplishments in a clear, concise fashion. This section becomes your personal skills inventory that can be tailored to match targeted career possibilities. Skills are divided into the following three categories:

 

FUNCTIONAL SKILLS:    Developed through experience, education/training and usually transferable to similar work environments.

 

ADAPTIVE SKILLS: Characteristics that aid ability to accept or adjust to changing work environments.

 

TRANSFERABLE SKILLS: Abilities developed than are applied across a variety of career roles.

IDENTIFYING AND DESCRIBING ACCOMPLISHMENTS

 

These practical examples of tangible results will differentiate you from others vying for similar opportunities. The most powerful way to convince a decision maker is to provide examples of how you have handled key responsibilities in the past. This section of your skills inventory will outline methods to identify and briefly summarize accomplishments and develop compact presentation outlines for each success story.

 

It is critical that each result is clearly measured and quantified whenever possible. Several examples include: reduced cost, meeting/exceeding time or dollar budget, increased productivity, new methods developed and time to complete project.

 

ACCOMPLISHMENT STATEMENT:

 

Developed preventative maintenance program that reduced system downtime 20%.

 

Implemented weekly department staff meeting which improved productivity 25%.

 

Developed a program to communicate sales leads which increased revenue $1 million per quarter.

 

Developed a new product launch program that increased market share 10% in one year.

 

Consolidated five operating functions into two which generated $500,000 annual savings.

 

Designed new product packaging system on time in 6 months and within the $100,000 budget.

 

FORMAT:

 

Begin with ACTION VERB, describe ACTIONS completed and RESULT, and conclude with measurement of result. Reconstruct the numbers to remain within a reasonable range based on scope of the enterprise and activity completed.

 

DEMONSTRATING VALUE

 

Use PAR method to develop presentation outline for each success story.

 

P: Problem or required action you faced.

A: Action Taken

R: Result achieved

 

The following is an example of each element of the PAR outline:

 

P: Support team was not responding to customers accurately or a timely manner.

 

A: Reference manual developed by team and approved by me which documented    

     common problems and best method to gain effective resolution.

            

R: Within three months after manual use implemented, the customer satisfaction

     improved 70% favorable to 88% favorable and resolution time was reduced

     30%.

 

The following is the summary placed on the resume as an accomplishment:

 

Developed customer response procedures manual that improved CSI 20% and    

productivity 30%.

 

COMPLETE INVENTORY OF ACCOMPLISHMENTS

 

Begin with your current position and describe at least 3 accomplishments you

completed in the last year and do the same for each of the previous four years.

 

Next describe at least 1 accomplishment for each of the previous five years

and at least 2 for any time earlier than ten years ago.

 

Finally develop a PAR outline for each success story.
