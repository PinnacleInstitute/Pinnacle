SUCCESSFUL NETWORKING

 

Networking is the most powerful career search channel in surfacing the “uncommon opportunity”. Most of us totally underestimate the volume of possible contacts that we have developed that can be leveraged into a very expansive database. Several results of this powerful process include: gaining access to decision makers, creating positions to match skills to organization needs, speeding the interview process, gaining valuable market information and enhancing market visibility. Clearly, networking becomes career market research that usually generates leads for potential career options.

Anyone you know for any reason can be a possible contact to provide information, suggestions, leads and introduce you to members of their network. As the career search candidate follows up on referral to associates of their contacts, the range of contacts expands geometrically. It is reasonable to project that the number of contacts will be increased by a factor of 10.

 

 

 

 

CREATE A DATA BASE

 

As the networking process moves forward, add the contact information for each new contact to an electronic data base and record in each contact field the contact connection (e.g., the name of the referral source). Much time and future effort will be saved if a complete data input is included at the time of contact. Once a system is in place, each career search candidate can confidently move forward with the process and begin to connect with “high comfort level” contacts. This will create a foundation so that each career search candidate can begin to move away from close contacts and begin to develop new relationships further away from their center of gravity.

 

NETWORKING VIA DISCUSSIONS AND MEETINGS

 

Before moving forward, develop a list of contacts and rank them as A (strong relationship, high probability that the person will generate leads/referrals/valuable information), B (moderate value) or C (low value but may be helpful). Once the ranking is complete, start reaching out to the A level contacts via email or phone and establish a convenient date/time to briefly discuss career exploration goals and plans.

 

The primary mission of a networking discussion is to gather valuable market data and gain feedback on the strategy developed. Also, a growing number of contacts are aware that someone they know and respect is exploring opportunities. Many begin to discover that this process of asking for assistance and information is not a sign of weakness. It demonstrates self confidence, a desire to learn and willingness to expand beyond a safe comfort zone.

 

Start each conversation or introductory message with a brief description of the career path options being explored and the reassurance that nothing is expected beyond helpful information (e.g., no specific job leads expected). Express appreciation for any suggestions provided and provide positive feedback on specific follow through on recommendations provided. In addition: develop and use a discussion outline for initial discussions, practice on close friends before moving forward and ask for feedback, remain relaxed and confident, start with name of referral source, keep the discussion as brief as possible, be patient and record key information and/or follow up actions.

 

Once this process becomes routine, it will become energizing, highly motivating and productive. When an individual gets beyond for fear of “reaching out”, the true value becomes very clear. A much larger segment of the work population now recognizes the tremendous value and power of networking than they did five to ten years ago and usually respond in a positive, helpful manner.

NETWORK MEETING PREPARATION

 

Learn or update information on contact prior to the discussion.

 

Prepare further questions and report on follow up actions completed.

 

Have clear meeting goals.

 

DURING THE NETWORKING MEETING

 

Establish rapport and let the contact turn to more formal discussion.

 

Establish meeting expectations and purpose.

 

Let the other person do 70-80 % of the talking.

 

Again, close with key points of information gained and further follow up plans.

 

AFTER THE MEETING

 

Send and thank you email or note.

 

Make sure that all information and follow up needed are clearly recorded.

 

CONTINUE THE PROCESS

 

Once every A and B individual have been contacted, begin to contact those individuals on each person’s referral list and continue to connect the “dots”. Most skilled networkers will reach out at least two levels beyond their primary contact. If an average of three new names is developed from each individual contacted, the size of your data base will quickly grow by a factor of ten (e.g., person #1=3

contacts, each of these 3=3 per contact, 3+(3x3)=12). Remember, the only control we have, in this process, is the act of reaching out. Good luck and enjoy!!
