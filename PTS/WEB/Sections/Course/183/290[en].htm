MANAGING CHANGE

 

 

THE CONSTANTS OF CHANGE:

 

·        You will change either by design or default

·        Change can be planned and managed

·        Both how and what you change are equally important

·        25% of all those employed change positions each year

·        On an average we all change jobs every three years

 

You are in the beginning phase of positive change and it is critical that we experience a range of emotions when experiencing change. When the change is a surprise to us, we will experience SHOCK followed by CONFUSION. If planned, we will become unsettled but the feelings will not be as extreme. During all periods of change, individuals feel ANGER or become UNSETTLED, then FEAR or UNCERTAINTY and then settle into a feeling of ACCEPTANCE. It is very normal to go through these phases of emotion so allow yourself to move through each stage. Please contact the instructor/course developer if you have any questions or concerns when working on this and each succeeding lesson (lbentley@organizationtransitionsinc.com).  

 

Deciding how your career will change will naturally create stress. As long as the stress does not become distress it can create positive energy. There are several courses of action that will support effective management of change and keep the positive energy flowing which include:

 

CREATING STRUCTURE

 

Your normal activities become comfortable because of familiarity and predictability. During period of change, create a new but similar routine. Get up at your usual time, plan your day, establish short term goals and track your progress. Make sure that you are managing time wisely and remain focused.

 

RECOGNIZE SUCCESS

 

Developing a career plan and exploring various options is a tedious process. Initially, focus on accomplishments that you can achieve (e.g., 10 phone calls per week), establish a series of building blocks and pat yourself on the back when you complete short range goals and approach the next check point in your chain of goals.

 

STAY CONNECTED

 

This is a time to reach out to others for ideas and support, open up to close friends explain the basics of the change process to family members. Spend time with the people who understand and accept you and will patiently listen to your true feelings. Do not limit your range of contacts and work on expanding your support group. Make sure you discuss your plans and progress with professional colleagues and increase the number of professional meetings you attend. Also, become more active in religious and/or community groups and explore networking groups that meet in your area. Be sure to seek out other people who are also exploring career options or searching for a new job. We all feel better when we eat a balanced diet, exercise regularly, and get a good amount of sleep.

 

CONSITENTLY REVIEW CAPABILITIES AND SUCCESSES

 

As you career exploration progresses, it is very easy to overlook progress and accomplishments. Take pride in each step you take and the tools you develop. Take pride in your past accomplishments and the tremendous inventory of skills you have developed. Taking command of your career becomes a very empowering experience.
