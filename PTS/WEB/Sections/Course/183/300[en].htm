MANAGEMENT TECHNIQUES FOR CAREER SEARCH

 

A well developed and diligently monitored performance management plan will pay handsome career success dividends. The following planning outline will provide an excellent model for precise results:

 

DESIRED CAREER SEARCH OBJECTIVES AND POTENTIAL OBSTACLES

 

Describe four to six career search objectives that will maximize success during the first three months of the campaign. Once you have achieved a reasonable comfort level with this process, establish realistic, longer range objectives.

 

Next to each objective list possible obstacles that could block achieving desired results. Examples could include difficulty contacting people that are strangers, lack of knowledge about search sources, lack of computer skills, or narrow career focus.

TRACK RESULTS

 

Now that objectives, possible obstacles and rewards have been established, results need to be evaluated every couple of weeks. It is critical that each target should be viewed as dynamic and make required adjustments based on actual outcomes. Where necessary, seek added clarification from trusted sources of input.

 

Take the time to think through issues and information received. Also keep in mind that this process is designed to achieve win-win outcomes. Make sure that steady progress is maintained.
