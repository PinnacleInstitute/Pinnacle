OPPORTUNITY FOLLOW UP

 

A follow up message your letter should be sent after any meeting, discussion or interview. Use this as a valuable communication tool to clarify information, confirm interest in a tangible opportunity and reinforce abilities that match any active opportunity.

Sample Message

 

Dear Mr./Ms. or first name if appropriate,

 

It was a pleasure to meet with you (date) to discuss possible opportunities with your company. Based on our discussion, the marketing manager position requirements you described match my skills and interests very well. The following are several examples:

 

My success developing creative market management programs that have increased company market share 10-20 %.

 

My ability to lead the development of new products that have increased revenue levels $10-20 million annually.

 

The very innovative marketing strategy that was developed for XYZ Company that led to the opening of a totally new market niche for the company.

 

Demonstrated success building and developing marketing and sales teams that delivered industry leading results.

 

Please let me know when you plan to schedule our next meeting and if you need any further information.

 

Best regards,

 

 

CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

 

During the career search research and evaluation process a variety of research and search management techniques has provided varying degrees of success. The following check list will guide search efforts:

 

How many hours per week have been devoted to career search efforts?

 

Has your contact data base increased in size and what is the degree of increase (Guideline: increase by a factor of 10 over 3 months)?

 

Have you explored opportunities in each of your career path option categories?

 

When following up with new contacts, what methods have worked best or poorly?

 

What has been the rough per cent positive response been from each of the search methods utilized?

 

Highlight factors used to drop pursuit of potential opportunities.

 

Have I demonstrated continuous progress based on meeting preparation and evaluation of results?

 

Highlight significant changes to career search plan and management methods.

 

What caused changes to marketing materials and presentation?

 

Has the preparation for any meeting included tailoring presentation of skills and accomplishments to match critical opportunity requirements or have I migrated to a more narrowly focused comfort zone?

 

Have effective follow up methods been applied to keep all potential/interesting opportunities active?

 

Are overall career interest patterns developing and is an updated ranking of options possible?

 

Remember that the only result that can be managed is the number of contacts initiated and the follow up applied. Remember to pat yourself on the back when you achieve search targets established!

 

Be sure to project the timing for a potential choice of the next best career step.

 

Each career search candidate is now prepared to prioritize career choices.
