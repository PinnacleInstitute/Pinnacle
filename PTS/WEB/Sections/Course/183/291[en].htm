SELF-ASSESSMENT

Step One to Exploring New Career Opportunities

 

Career exploration candidates often shortchange themselves because they do not give the evaluation process enough time or effort. People who are considering career options often become confused and emotional because the change environment is viewed as being complex, uncertain and, at times, overwhelming. Most overlook this critical first step: understanding who they are and what they want. Insight is lacking because the required questions have not been asked and many attributes are overlooked.

 

These considerations becomes “emotionally charged” because our culture often links people with their career identity. Individuals are evaluating one of their basic emotional pillars and often shy away from careful analysis. Everyone has a unique combination of primary values, skills, interests, behaviors and motivations that need to be identified and summarized.

 

The following process of self-assessment builds a critical foundation too often taken for granted. One of the most common road blocks to successful career management is caused by the inability to communicate a clear link between relevant capabilities and top priority career path requirement. To build this multifaceted description you will complete a series of exercises as thoroughly, honestly and precisely as possible. There are no right or wrong answers.

CHALLENGE:        A stimulating, interesting activity or problem.

 

EARNINGS:            Income level that enables you to live beyond the essentials of life.

ENVIROMENT:      Satisfying surroundings in a healthy physical and emotional

 

FAMILY:                 Time to maintain close immediate family ties.

INTELLECTUAL STIMULATION: Work involving creative use of rational and         mature thought.

LOCATION:                               Live in or close to a specific geographic region.

 

PHILOSOPHY:                           Latitude to hold personal values without work conflict.

 

POWER:                                      Control or influence over the work or goal definitions of others.

 

RECOGNITION:                          Positive feedback, public credit for achievements.

 

SECURITY:                                 Stable career environment with reasonable financial rewards for accomplishments.

 

STATUS:                                      Prestige and recognition from family, friends and community based on your career field.

 

TRAVEL:                                     Opportunity to travel as part of the job.

 

VARIETY:                                    Diverse responsibilities with reasonably frequent changes in activities or setting.

CAREER RESILIANCE QUESTIONAIRE

 

Respond to the following statements using a 1 to 5 scale: 1- “Do not agree at all” 3- “Agree somewhat” 5- “Strongly agree”

 

I can easily describe the value I bring to an employer.

 

During an interview, I could list at least ten skills I possess.

 

My career goals are clear and I have a plan to achieve them.

 

I can clearly summarize three important accomplishments from my most recent job.

 

My skill level has grown during the last two years.

 

I have increased my computer skills during the last year.

 

I track industry trends and have identified current and projected changes.

 

I look for new and interesting activities in my work area.

 

I actively contribute to team activities and support achieving mutual goals.

 

The skills and abilities needed to continue my career growth are clear to me.

 

I have a very solid and active career “support group”.

 

I have actively sought growth assignments in my current and past jobs.

 

I constantly seek career expanding information through contacts, reading and professional meetings.

 

I thoroughly enjoy the constantly changing world of work.
