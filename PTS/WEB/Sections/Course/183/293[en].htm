CAREER SEARCH STRATEGY AND ACTION PLAN

 

Before launching a career search it is very important to determine the number and types of career paths to explore. Most tend to move through career steps in a very narrowly focused manner. This program provides an opportunity to broaden potential options and explore multiple possibilities.

 

There are four basic categories that can be utilized to organize search targets: similar types of positions held during your career in similar industries; similar types of positions in a wider variety of industries; opportunities where abilities, knowledge and interests are utilized in different ways; and totally new career path such as business venture or new opportunity that requires additional education.

 

The following guidelines will help focus your thoughts and provide a template for a creative career exploration strategy:

 

OPTION #1

 

List companies that represent the industry segment/s where you have gained most of you career experience.

 

Examples: 

Consumer Products- Gillette, P&G, Revlon, Pillsbury

 

Pharmaceuticals- Merck, Pfizer, Glaxo/SmithKline

 

Communications- AT&T, Sprint,Verizon

 

Software- Microsoft, Oracle, 3Com

 

Consider the following questions: Will this option still work for me, who can help my exploration and what are possible obstacles.

 

OPTION #2

 

List companies that represent other industry segments where you could transition your experience and knowledge. (Consider questions from Option #1)

 

Examples:

Consumer Products- Software, Cell Phones, Education

 

Pharmaceuticals- Chemicals, Consumer Products, Medical Services

 

Communications- Internet Services, Network Software, Marketing Services

 

Software- Internet Services, Hardware, Consulting Services

 

OPTION #3

 

List your skills, interests and knowledge that could be utilized in other functional areas of responsibility. (Consider questions from Option #1)

 

Examples:

Marketing Management- Sales, Customer Support, Strategic Planning

 

Operations Supervisor- Field Support, Materials Planning, Purchasing

 

Information Systems Analyst- Business Analyst, Network Admin., Recruiter

 

Cost Accountant- Financial Analysis, Systems Support, Business Planning

 

HR Manager- Operations Manager, Sales Manager, Customer Support

 

Business Management- Consultant, Consulting Sales, Project Manager

 

List your skills, knowledge, interests and areas of expertise that could be transferred to the alternate functional areas of responsibility identified:

 

Examples:

People Management, Customer Knowledge, Product Knowledge, Budget Development/Management, Presentation Skills and Adaptive Skills.

 

OPTION #4

 

Consider creative “new” career path options such as entrepreneurial business venture, career requiring added education, focus on an area of personal interest. Take the time to visualize dream job possibilities and identify information required to further evaluate prospects. (Consider questions from Option #1)

 

Examples:

Talk to successful business owners.

 

Gather information on education requirements.

 

Consider shifting a hobby to a career activity.

 

Develop outline for book idea.

 

Volunteer to learn more about area of interest.

 

This option should be evaluated along with the first three more pragmatic options.
