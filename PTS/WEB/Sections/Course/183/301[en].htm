During The Interview:

 

Greet the interviewer with a firm hand shake and upbeat manner. Remain very responsive and interactive during introductory casual discussion (e.g., “How long have you lived in this area”? Answer, then you ask, “How long have you lived in the area?”).

 

Present information and participate in the more formal discussion with a calm, profession, informed manner and interact with the interviewer in a similar fashion to the way you conduct discussions with professional associates.

 

Listen very carefully to gather other critical information that can help you further refine your presentation and demonstrate that you are absorbing the questions and information being presented.

 

Be positive, open and “to the point” and do not oversell skills and capabilities. The initial response should be a brief overview of critical information to let the interviewer probe if more detail is needed. Probe or clarify as needed.

SAMPLE INTERVIEW QUESTIONS

 

Tell me a little about yourself (One to two minute response and use resume Career Summary as a guide).

 

Describe the working relationship you had with your most recent manager.

 

Are areas of your current job that you do not enjoy?

 

What do you consider to be your strongest assets?

 

Are there skills that you need to improve?

 

If you could change, what areas of your career growth would you alter?

 

Describe your ideal job.

 

What motivates you most?

 

How would your manager describe you?

 

What interests you most about our company (a great area to demonstrate research completed)?

 

What are you longer range career goals?

 

What are your salary requirements?

 

In your most recent position, what did you consider to be your greatest accomplishment?

 

What were the circumstances behind your last two job changes?

 

Please describe your most significant job failure.

 

Describe the most difficult person you have ever worked with.

 

What types of responsibilities are most difficult for you?

 

Why are you leaving your current company?

 

SAMPLE QUESTIONS TO ASK INTERVIEWER

 

Remember that every interview is a mutual opportunity for the candidate and interviewer to gain critical information. It is important to ask a few key questions during the interview with a larger portion being asked in the opening formal phase of the interview. The candidate should be asking roughly one question for every six to eight questions asked by the interviewer.

 

Why is this position available?

 

Are there any particular reasons why the last person left this position?

 

What skills are most critical to success in this role?

 

Have any key goals been established for the individual that assumes this position?

 

In reviewing company data, I observed that you lead your industry. How do you plan to remain the leader?

 

What types of support does the person in this position receive?

 

What is a logical next career step for a person in this role?

 

How is success measured for employees?

 

What has most significantly impacted organization success?

 

What types of business or organization changes do you foresee?

 

Will there be career and/or skill development available to aid career growth?

 

Are there any further questions you would like to ask me?

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

NON VERBAL COMMUNICATION

 

Review the following factors that have been highlighted and ranked from negative to positive and excessive:

 

Energy: Very low, flat voice; positive, upbeat expression; loud, rambling tone

 

Confidence: Nervous habits; relaxed and energetic; poor posture

 

Friendly: Simple yes or no answers; pleasant facial expression; too familiar

 

Sincerity: Poor eye contact; relaxed eye contact; forced stare

 

Attitude: Arms folded; open posture; excessive laughing

 

Attention: Eyes down; decisive, relaxed hand gestures; rapid delivery

 

Polish: Out of date dress, poorly shined shoes; dress appropriate; too flashy

 

Delivery: Very stiff posture; good posture and lean forward periodically; too close

 

Professionalism: Halting speech; precise delivery; disorganized delivery

 

CLOSE INTERVIEW POSITIVELY

 

As interview closes, briefly summarize how your skills and interests match the position needs and requirements.

 

Demonstrate interest and provide positive feedback on the meeting.

 

Determine next steps in the process and timing.
