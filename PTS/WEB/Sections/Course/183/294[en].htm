CAREER EXPLORATION MARKETING PLAN

 

When a well developed strategy has been completed, the time is right for comprehensive market research. To market a product successfully, a detailed marketing plan must be developed. At this point in the career exploration process, each individual needs to view themselves as a “product”. Evaluating and selecting the next best career step is one of the most challenging marketing efforts to be accomplished in any environment. This marketing plan greatly enhances opportunities for success!

 

To complete the plan, consider the following questions:

 

What/where is the target market?

What is the demand level for key skills and experience?

Who is the decision maker?

What is the best channel to reach the decision maker?

 

Other considerations are:

 

Determining resources required and budget parameters;

establishing a time line from beginning to completion;

determining the methods to promote the product.

 

With these questions and considerations in mind and the career search strategy as a launch pad, use the following guidelines to develop your marketing plan:

 

Develop a target list of contacts that should be able to provide market data, feedback on your strategy and refer you to other potential information sources. The following contact information should be recorded:

 

Networking Contact

Phone/email

Company

Name of Decision Maker

 

PLAN OUTLINE

 

Finalize your career search objectives and target position specifications including top priority responsibilities, compensation range, work environment, career values.

 

Next list your key attributes that sell you best into primary functional and industry targets.

 

Identify and list the possible objections or barriers to overcome. Set an objective to test your list to ensure they are relevant.

 

DETERMINE REQUIRED SUPPORT FROM OTHERS (Rate high, medium, or low)

 

Finalize strategy and set objectives

Career search skill training

Resume/communication methods development

Market research

Interview preparation

Decision making

Other possible support
