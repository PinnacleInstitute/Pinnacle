COURSE OUTLINE

1. The nature of social aggregates / social groups

You will learn about the type and nature of various social groupings.

2. Social Groups

You will examine what a social group is in terms of its characteristics and properties.

3. Primary Groups

You will be able to identify the basic characteristics of a small group and its importance.

4. Secondary Groups

You will be able to identify the nature of formally organized groups.

5. The group environment

You will be able to understand that groups function in two main environments: the one they create for themselves and the one in which they functioning.

6. Why study groups are important

This will enable you to discover the importance of groups in all life spheres and why it is important to study group behaviour.

Course Goals

The aim of this course is to enable you to 1. understand the types of social groupings in terms of their nature and structure. 2. differentiate between primary and secondary groups. 3. identify the nature of both the group's internal and external environment and to obtain a brief orietation of the impact of these environments on each other. 4. understand the importance of groups in your life and why it is important to study group behaviour.
