Placing a Table into a Word Document

     It is really handy to be able to use a table to organize a section of your Word document. To insert a table into your document, do the following:
  1. Click on the insert table button on the toolbar.
  2. After clicking on the table button, a grid emerges. This grid is used to tell the computer how many rows and columns you want your table to have. Remember, rows are horizontal and columns are vertical!
  3. In this example, here is the table specifications I want for my table.
  4. After I have made my specifications for my table, I simply click my mouse button for the table to appear on my Word document.
  5. The table is now in my Word document. When the table emerges, the cursor sits in the first cell. To move the cursor to the next cell, hit the tab button. To bring the cursor back to a previous cell, hit shift-tab.

The Invisible Table

     Invisible tables are used to enahance a document's layout. To insert and invisible table, do the following:

  1. Place a table into your Word document
  2. Highlight the entire table
  3. Click on the black arrow just outside the border button
  4. Choose the no border selection and click on it
  5. You now have a border with grey borders. The grey color indicates that the borders will not appear on paper when the document is printed.

Changing Bullets or Numbering

     Changing bullet or numbering style is simple. Word 97 offers you a number of different bullets and numbering styles to choose from.

  1. On the menu bar, go to format and then select Bullets and Numbering
  2. Next, select the bullet or number style that you like and then click on OK
  3. Once that is done, you can add your text and everytime you enter the enter key, a bullet or number of your style will appear indented on the next line.

Creating Outlines

     Outlines can be both simple and complex. By creating your outline with minimal use of the arrow keys, your job will be simple. By using the arrow keys, creating your outline can become very difficult! To create a great-looking outline with minmal frustration, do the following:

  1. On the menu bar, go to format and then click on bullets and numbering
  2. Click on the outline numbered tab.
  3. Select the style you want and click on OK
  4. To indent (to move the line ahead) use the tab key
  5. To un-indent (to move the line back), hold down the shift key while hitting the tab button.

Columns

     Columns create a great look for your document and they are a key element in desktop publishing. To create columns in your document, do the following:

  1. On the menu bar, go to format and then select columns

  2. Next, select the number of columns you want in your document. The preview window shows you what the document will look like. To add a line between columns, add a check next to the line between statement.

  3. Lastly, begin to type your text. Notice how Word 97 handles the text within your columns.

Sections of a Document with and without columns

     Adding columns to only certain sections of a document can be tricky, but it is not too difficult if you do things in order one at a time. Here is how it is done.

  1. Center your heading in the middle of the page. Put the cursor on a new line.

  2. On the menu bar, go to format and then select columns.

  3. Select the number of columns you want.

  4. Put a check next to line between

  5. Now, in the apply to field, change to this point forward.

  6. You can now type your text and Word 97 will squeeze your text into the columns without affecting your title at the top of the page.

Inserting a Footer with the Page Number

     A footer with the page numbers changing automatically is incredibly useful in a Word document. Do the following:

  1. Click on view and select header and footer
  2. Click on the switch between header and footer button
  3. Activate the right alignment button and type your footer text in the footer box.
  4. Click on the add page number button.
  5. Once you see the page number next to your footer text, click on close on the footer options menu bar.
  6. At the bottom of the page, you will now see the footer and the page number in grey text.
