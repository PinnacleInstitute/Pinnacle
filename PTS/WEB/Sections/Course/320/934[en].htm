Indenting a Paragraph

     Using Word, indenting a paragraph is very simple. By placing the insertion point anywhere within the parargraph that you wish to indent, one click moves the entire paragraph.

     To indent a paragraph using the increase indent button:

  1. Make sure the insertion point is located anywhere within the paragraph that you wish to indent.
  2. Click the increase indent button on the formatting toolbar. The entire paragraph moves right 0.5" each time you click the increase indent button.

  3. Click the decrease indent button on the formatting toolbar to move the paragraph left 0.5".

 

Using Format Painter

     The Format Painter makes it easy to copy all the formatting features of one paragraph to one or more other paragraphs. In this lesson, you will use Format Painter now to copy the formatting of the second paragraph to the fourth paragraph.  You will begin by highlighting the paragraph whose format you want to copy.

  1. Highlight the second paragraph by holding down the mouse button while dragging it until the entire paragraph is highlighted (or click three times on the second paragraph).

  2. Double-click the format painter icon

  3. Now, click anywhere in the fourth paragraph and it will automatically become formatted the same way as the second paragraph.

  4. After formatting the paragraph, you may have to click the format painter icon one time to turn off the feature.

Adding Bullets and Numbers

     Bullets and numbers are useful when you need to emphasize a particular list of items.

     To apply numbers to a list:

  1. Highlight the list of items that you wish to be numbered.
  2. Click the numbering button on the formatting toolbar. Consecutive numbers appear in front of each item in the list.

     To apply bullets to a list:

  1. Highlight the list of items that you wish to be bulleted.
  2. Click on the bullet button on the formatting toolbar. A rounded bullet, a special character, appears in front of each item.

Emphasizing Text with Boldface, Underlining and Italics

     You can emphasize words in your document with boldface, underlining or italics. These styles help you make specific thoughts, ideas, words or phrases stand out.

     To change the font style to boldface:

  1. Highlight the word, phrase, sentence or paragraph that you would like boldfaced.

  2. Click on the boldface button and the highlighted text is now boldfaced

     To underline text:

  1. Highlight the text that you want to underline

  2. Click on the underline button and highlighted text is now underlined.

     To italicize text:

  1. Highlight the text that you want to italicize.

  2. Click on the italicize button and the text is now italicized.
