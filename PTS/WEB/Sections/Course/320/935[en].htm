Formatting the Document in Sections

     The title page of the multi-page report should be centered between the top and bottom margins of the page. In order to format the title page differently from the rest of the report, you need to divide the document into sections. A section is a unit or part of a document that can have its own page orientation, margins, headers, footers and vertical alignment. Each section, in other words, is like a mini-document within a document.

     To divide a document into sections, you insert a section break, a dotted line with the words "End of Section" that marks the point at which one section ends and another begins. Sections can start on a new page or continue on the same page. The easiest way to insert a section break is to use the Break command on the Insert menu.

     To insert a section break after the title:

  1. Position the insertion point immediately to the left of the "E" in the heading "Executive Summary." You want the text above this heading to be on a separate title page and the executive summary to begin on the second page of the report.

  2. Click insert on the menu bar, ad then click break to open the Break dialog box.

  3. Click the next page option button in the Section breaks area, and then click the OK button. A double-dotted line and words "End of Section" appear before the heading "Executive Summary," indicating that you have inserted a section break.

     Now that the title page is a separate section and page from the rest of the report, you can make changes affecting only that section, leaving the rest of the document in tact.

Changing the Vertical Alignment of a Section

     You can change the vertical alignment to center the lines of text between the top and bottom margins. The vertical alignment specifies how a page of text is positioned on the page between the top and bottom margins- flush at the top, flush at the bottom, or centered between the top and bottom margins.

     To change the vertical alignment of the title page:

  1. Place the insertion point anywhere within the title page.
  2. Click file on the menu bar, and then click page setup to open the Page Setup dialog box.

  3. Click the layout tab if it is not already selected. In the Apply to list box, click This Section if it is not already selected so that the layout change affects only the first section, not both sections, of your document.

  4. Click the vertical alignment list arrow, and then click center to center the pages of the current selection vertically between the top and bottom margins.

  5. Click the OK button. The text of the title page is centered vertically as shown below.

     You have successfully centered the title page text. Next, you turn your attention to placing descriptive name for the report and the page number at the top of every page.

Adding Headers

     Text that is printed at the top of every page is called a header. Similary, a footer is text that is printed at the bottom of every page.

     When you insert a header or footer into a document, you switch to Header and Footer view. The Header and Footer toolbar is displayed and the insertion point moves to the top of the document, where the header will appear. The main text is dimmed, indicating that it cannot be edited until you return to normal or page layout view.

     You will create a header for the main body of the report (section 2) that prints "EverRipe Recommendation Report" at the left margin and the page number at the right margin.

  1. Make sure that the insertion point is anywhere within the second section of the report.

  2. Click view on the menu bar and then click header and footer. The screen changes to header and footer view and the header and footer toolbar appears in the document window. The header area appears in the top margin of your document surrounded by a dashed line.

  3. Click the same as previous button on the header and footer toolbar so that the button is not pressed. This ensures that the text of the current header will apply only to the current section (section 2) and not to the the previous section (section 1).
  4. Type EverRipe Recommendation Report. The title is automatically aligned on the left.

  5. Press the tab key twice to move the insertion point to the right margin of the header area. Type the word Page and press the space bar once.

  6. Click the insert page number button on the header and footer tool bar. The page number "2" appears at the right-aligned tab.

  7. Click the close button on the header and footer toolbar to return to normal view.

     Notice that you cannot see the header in normal view. To see exactly how the header will appear on the printed page, you can switch to page layout view, which lets you read headers and footers as well as see the margins. To do so:

  1. Click the page layout view button located at the bottom left-hand side of the screen.

     The report now has a header. Your next job is to make style changes throughout the document.

Attaching a Template to a Document

     You can change the available styles by using a different template. A template is a set of predefined styles designed for a specific type of document. For example, Word provides templates for formatting reports, brochures, memos, letters, or resumes among others. Word's default template, the Normal template, contains the Normal paragraph style.

     There are two steps to using a template. First, you need to attach the template to the document. Then you need to apply the template's styles to the various parts of the document. You will begin by attaching a new template to the EverRipe Report document.

     To preview and attach the Professional Report template to your document:

  1. Click format on the menu bar, and then click style gallery to open the style gallery dialog box. Your document appears in the preview window. The report appears formatted the same way as it is in the document window.

  2. Scroll to and then click professional report in the templates list box to select the template. In the preview report window, the text of your document changes to reflect the new normal style for the professional report template.

  3. In the preview box, click the example option button to see a sample document that uses all the professional report template styles. Scroll through the sample document to preview all the styles that are available to you in this template.

  4. Click OK to attach the template to the report and return to the document window. The template's default font (12 point Arial) and paragraph indentation are applied to the entire document, because the text in the document has been formatted with the normal style, and because no other styles have been applied at this point.

  5. Click the style list arrow on the formatting toolbar. Scroll through the style list box to verify that the styles of the professional report template are now available in this document, and then click the style list arrow again to close the style list.

Applying Styles

     The best way to apply a template's styles to a document is to highlight individual parts of the document, and then select the appropriate style from the style list on the formatting toolbar. For example, to format the report title, you would highlight "Increasing the Profitability of AgriTechnology's EverRipe Tomatoes" on the title page, and then select the Title Cover style from the style list.

To apply styles to the report document:

  1. Scroll to the title page and then drag the pointer to select the title Increasing the Profitability of AgriTechnology's EverRipe Tomatoes.

  2. Click the style list arrow on the formatting toolbar to open the style list. Scroll down the list, and then click Title Cover. Word applies the style to the selected text.
