Internet Promotions - 5 Great Ways To Promote Your Lead Program Online!


Method #1.  Affiliate Partners

You can greatly leverage your time and effort by partnering with others who cater to your target market and offer complimentary products or services.  Affiliate marketing is one of the most effective online marketing techniques available on the Internet today.  It is second only to Search Engine Optimization (SEO).

Provide some form of reward for your affiliate partner's referrals, typically a revenue share or referral fee.  You only need to compensate your affiliate partners when their lead becomes a customer.  This is a very low risk method that eliminates all the guess work in promotions that may or may not produce customers.

Method #2.  Pay-Per-Click

Pay-Per-Click advertising can be a very cost effective method at generating traffic for a lead program.

Pay-Per-Click advertising is just as it sounds.  You pay for each click they deliver to you.  This is usually quite cheap starting at only $.01 per click with many different places.  Others like Google will charge a minimum $.05 per click.  With PPC advertising you need to create a list of keywords and phrases that are closely related to your site or product.  You would enter these into your PPC account.  Then depending on the amount you had bid for each keyword your ad will be displayed through their network anytime someone searches your term. 

Some phrases and keywords can be very competitive and really tough to get into with a small budget.  However, with that being said this method can be very effective because your reaching people at the exact moment that they are looking for your product or keywords.


Method #3.  Opt-In Email & Newsletters

Online newsletters also work extremely well for promoting your lead program to your target market.  They can be sent for low cost and you will never have to worry about SPAM complaints as these people are active subscribers of the particular company.

Look for websites that offer complimentary products and services who have an option on their website to subscribe to their newsletter or sign-up for information notifications.  These companies typically sell advertising space in their emails and newsletters.

The advertisment must entice the viewer to click through to your lead program. Lead with your unique selling proposition and the key benefit to your targeted market niche.       
   
Online newsletters are also known as ezines. Most newsletters sell their ad space at rates  that are very affordable. Many starting at less than $15 for a classified style ad. In most newsletters your ad can can appear within just days of placing it. Sometimes you'll  manage to get a turnaround as quick as the same day.
   
Here's list to help you get started finding newsletters that target the people you want to talk to. For even more results just go to Google.com and search for 'ezines' and 'ezine advertising'.      

  • Ezine Universe: http://ezine-universe.com
  • The Ezine Directory: http://www.ezine-dir.com 
  • Cumuli: http://www.cumuli.com/ezines
  • Ezine Hub: http://www.ezinehub.com 
  • Web Scout Lists: http://www.webscoutlists.com 
  • Ezine Links: http://www.ezinelinks.com

There are three types of ads that ezine publishers usually offer:
    
1) Classified Ad
This is typically a small 3 to 5 line ad that appears either at the beginning, middle or end of the ezine. These are the most affordably priced ads.
    
2) Sponsorship
This is usually a much larger ad at (or near) the very top of the ezine. It usually costs a little more than a simple classified ad.
    
3) Stand-alone Or Solo Mailing
When you do a solo mailing your message gets mailed to all the ezine subscribers all by itself; just your ad and nothing else. It's the most expensive of the three options. However, depending on the circulation of the ezine, this could be as low as $35.00 to about 5,000 people.
    
When you're writing an ad for an ezine remember that you will only be able to use text. Keep it short, exciting and full of benefits. Don't forget a strong call to action.

 
Method #4. Forum Posting.  Free & It Works

Where else can you easily contact thousands of people from all walks of life from all over the globe? And all with just a few mouse clicks!
 
The Internet has one particular gathering place that's the online equivalent of the office water cooler or talking to your neighbor over the backyard fence.
 
The place is called a Discussion Forum. It's not one single place. There are literally thousands of online Forums dedicated to almost any topic you can think of.
 
They are a great place to make friends and build up your contacts, and thus people to prospect.
 
To get an idea of how much business discussion is going on out there just go to Google and search for "MLM Forum."
 
There will be over 1 million results from your search!
 
Read through the posts on a few and see which ones match your personality and seem interesting to you. Register to participate in two or three to start with.
 
Spend some time just reading and watching for awhile. Don't just jump in and start posting. Get a feel for the community and what 'is' and what 'is not' considered polite and acceptable topics for discussion.
 
Try to get known as someone who is knowledgeable and willing to help in any way you can. People will begin to view you as a community leader and they will ask you about your business.
 
Forum posting is definitely time consuming but has proven itself as one of the most effective free methods of generating real targeted traffic to a site.  It's as easy as finding a forum or discussion board related to your industry and then start contributing to the discussions.

There are several for the MLM/Network Marketing industry.  When joining the forums please pay careful attention to your signature.  In most cases this is the only way you can advertise.  A signature is just a blurb that you create that will be at the tail of every one of your posts.  You can include your lead program URL as well.  Add to conversations whenever you can or just start asking questions. 

The amount of traffic will not be staggering if you only post in one forum.  Become active in a few different forums and be sure to read the posting rules.  Before you start to post.

 

Method #5. Viral Marketing  (Email Signature).

Viral Marketing is getting others to share your message with others who share your message, and so on, so that it spreads like a virus. One of the best vehicles for viral marketing is your own personal emails.  Your emails are sometimes forwarded to others who also forward it to others.

This is one of the most unused and misused online strategies. It applies both to online Forum posting and the e-mail you send out from your computer.
 
An Email Signature is nothing more than a short snippet of text that goes either at the end of your Forum post or at the end of your e-mail.
 
Email signatures are very much like your business card in the offline world. If you're sending e-mail messages without a signature you're missing a powerful and FREE opportunity to promote your business.
 
Start by preparing a brief message to append to the bottom of all your outgoing e-mail messages. It can include whatever you like, but the more useful things are your name, web site address, telephone and perhaps a 'teaser line' to get people's curiosity fired up and
motivate them to visit your web site or e-mail you.
 
 Here's an example of a simple:
 
 ==============================
 John Rep
 (555) 555-5555
 
 Fire Your Boss! Do It Today!
 http://www.YouShouldBeFree.com
 ==============================
 
Distinguish your signature. Whether you separate it from the body of your letter with a line or enclose it with a box of asterisks is a personal matter. Just be sure it's clear that it's separate from the body of your message. Give it its own presence. However, don't go to the opposite extreme.
 
Some signatures are designed to look like some kind of artwork or cartoon. The trouble is, by the time it gets to the computer it's all messed up. It ends up looking like a monkey came along and started pounding on the keyboard.
 
Many also seem to believe that abundantly applying dollar signs ($$$$$$) or TYPING IN ALL CAPS will make a lasting impression. They're right, it will...a long lasting bad impression. Also, this may be mistakenly considered to be junk e-mail and quickly thrown away.
 
In some cases, future mail from you may simply be filtered directly into the trash; a personal 'black hole' made just for you.
 
So, how do you make one and use it?
 
1. Check the e-mail software you're using. There should be an option for "Create Signature" or "Create Signature Stamp" or some similar wording.
 
2. Follow the instructions provided to create the file.
 
3. Your signature file should be brief (50 words/less).
 
4. It should provide contact information including some of the following: your name, web site, e-mail, fax on demand, phone, fax, etc.
 
5. It should provide at least one or two strong reasons for people to want to visit your site when they read the signature. This might be with a special offer, or a sentence or two about what they get if they visit your site, etc.
 
Now, whenever you send out an e-mail message be sure your signature file is attached. It saves you from having to type the same information over and over again.
 
Even if you're sending a simple, non-business e-mail to your Aunt Sally, make sure that your signature is on there. She just might ask what you're up to and what that 'stuff' at the bottom of your e-mail means.
 
Hah! A chance to tell her about your business. Okay, it might end up being just for practice, but who knows.
 
Now let's say you e-mailed Aunt Sally a joke you read and thought she might enjoy. Well, it turns out she liked it very much. So what does she do? She sends it out to everybody on her address list.
 
One of the people on her list (Bob) happens to participate in an e-mail discussion list with about five hundred members worldwide. Dave decides to forward the joke to the entire five hundred member discussion list.
 
Before you know it, someone in Australia is reading your joke...and your email signature!
 
Are you seeing the light now?  Make Sure Your Signature Is On EVERY E-Mail That Leaves Your Computer!
