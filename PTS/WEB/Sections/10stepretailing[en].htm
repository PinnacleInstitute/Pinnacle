Ten Step Retailing Sales Campaign

 

Step 1: Share The Benefit

Identify potential prospects and share the benefits of the product. Based on your available resources you can share the benefits of your product using various methods. You can share the benefits through directly distributing collateral materials to individuals with a need for the benefits of your product.  Collateral materials may include the company literature, DVDs, brochures, flyers or your website.  You may also choose to advertise your benefits to your target market using the appropriate media that targets your market like magazine ads, direct mailers, Internet ads or Internet search engine keywords.

The key to successfully promoting the product is identifying your target market and focusing your message on the benefit that they desire from the product. Do not be tempted to share benefits that they are not looking for, like other product lines or the business opportunity. You must stay focused.

Measure the results of your promotion efforts to determine the best methods and return on investment for reaching your target market.

Step 2: Qualify The Prospect

Determine the level of interest and need of your potential prospect. Contact each potential prospect immediately while their interest is still high. Your goal is not to convince them to proceed, but to determine if they are qualified to proceed. Do they have a need for the product? What exactly is their need? Do they have sufficient resources to purchase your product? This is a tricky question that you can’t ask outright. But you can ask other questions that build rapport and indirectly answer the question like What kind of work do you do? or What line of work is your spouse in?

This step is also the time to analyze the potential prospects needs. You must understand their specific needs and concerns before you can proceed to the next step. You can only present a solution to their problem if you completely understand their problem.

The most successful salespeople practice the art of listening to the prospect with probing questions so that they can later apply their solution to the prospect’s specific need. Remember, you have two ears and one mouth, use them proportionately.  Do not be tempted to do a product feature dump on your potential prospect. Listen for the benefits that they are looking for.

Step 3: Present The Solution

Support your value proposition with applicable experience and expertise. After you fully understand the prospect’s specific needs and concerns you can then present your product as a solution to their needs. Of course, only if it really is a solution to their problem. You will be further ahead in reputation and integrity if you can honestly admit if your product is not the right solution.

Bring in the experts if necessary to assist you with the presentation of the solution. Solicit help from your up-line sponsors; invite your prospect to listen to an expert speaker on a conference call or at a meeting; share additional information with them in audio or video format that confirms how your product can solve their problem.

Step 4: Experience The Product

Let the prospect experience the benefits of the product. Use the puppy-dog sales technique; leave your product with them to try.  If it solves their problem they will fall in love with your “puppy-dog”. Provide sufficiently brief instructions on how to use the product to maximize the benefit that they desire, but don’t overwhelm them with a detailed review of the entire owners manual. It needs to appear as simple as possible for them to receive their desired benefit.

Set the expectation with them that you will get back with them in a few days to get their feedback.

Step 5: Close The Sale

Invite the prospect to purchase the product.  Don’t be afraid to ask for the order. If they express satisfaction or excitement with experiencing the product then assume the sale. Ask them how they would like to pay for it.  “We take credit cards or personal checks, which would be more convenient for you?”

If they are not satisfied with experiencing the product you should continue to ask probing questions to confirm that you understand their expectations and that they used the product correctly. If necessary, address the issues and leave your “puppy-dog” product with them a little longer.

NOTE : After the prospect commits to purchasing your product, your work has just begun. You now need to follow steps 6 – 10 to assure customer satisfaction and more importantly, to convert your new customer into an advocate that will talk about the your product everywhere they go and will give you golden referrals.

Step 6: Product Training

When retailing the product make sure your customer knows how to the product.  Spend the time to insure they know all of the products features and functions. With Air product especially, educate your customer on how to adjust the units purification settings. Your customer also needs to be educated about the Nutrition products and their use. Training your prospect will increase the customers desire to continue the use of the product and increase your chances of gaining referrals.

Step 7: Show Appreciation

Your customer wants to know you appreciate their business. Thanking your customer with a card, phone call, or even compensation for a referral, not only helps your customer feel good about their purchase but will increase your chances of future business and referrals.

Step 8: Expectation Follow-up

During the demonstration or purchase, your customer sets their expectations of what they feel the product will do for them. Record these expectations and follow-up with specific questions directed toward their expectations.  Your customer is more likely to share their satisfaction or concerns if you are specific. Also make sure you leave your contact information with the customer, on the unit or in some other form making it ease for the customer to contact you with any questions.

  Step 9: Maintenance Follow-up

Any product you sell will need some maintenance follow-up. For example: You should  follow-up in 30 days, with air products, to help walk them through the cleaning of their unit, this will reduce there frustration and educate them for future cleanings. This helps you customer feel you are interested in their successful use of the product and gives them confidence in your ability to service the people they may refer you to.

Step 10: Request Referrals

Referrals are the life blood of retailing. People love to refer products and services that will not jeopardize their integrity and relationships. We all like to look good thus the referrals we receive must be handled with great care and diligence. Request referrals only after you have built a relationship of trust with your customer. This will increase the quality of the referrals and the customers willingness to provide them to you.
