After a variety of jobs ranging from teaching school to delivering ice, Jack Gallagher started his comedy career in Boston during the early 80's. As part of that explosive comedy scene, Jack helped establish such clubs as the Comedy Connection, Stitches, and the legendary Ding-Ho. He quickly established himself up and down the East Coast working clubs and colleges.


In 1984 Jack moved to Los Angeles where he quickly became a regular at The Improvisation in Hollywood.


Jack added big screen credits to his resume by landing a major role in the feature film Shakes the Clown written and directed by Bob “Bobcat” Goldthwait. He has also appeared in Heartbreak Ridge with Clint Eastwood and was featured in the made-for-television movie Incident at Ruby Ridge.


Gallagher has starred in his own ABC sitcom Bringing up Jack, appeared in a guest role on Cheers!, and recently spent a season as a writer on The Gregory Hines show.


A favorite of talk/variety show hosts and audiences, Jack has appeared on the Dennis Miller Show, Late Night with Conan O’Brien, Arsenio Hall as well as regular appearances on The Tonight Show with both Johnny Carson and Jay Leno.


Jack has headlined virtually every major comedy club in the country including The Improv, Catch a Rising Star, and The Punchline.


In addition, he has appeared in concert with such diverse acts as Dolly Parton (Trump Plaza), The Pointer Sisters (Caesars Palace), The Four Tops, Tony Bennett, Paul Anka, and Warren Zevon.


Jack has written and continues to perform his critically acclaimed one-man show Letters to Declan in theaters and at corporate events across the country. He also speaks on a variety of topics ranging from “Customer Service” to “Adapting to Change” with both humor and insight.


Since 1990, Jack has appeared in television and radio commercials and on award winning outdoor billboard campaigns representing Sacramento based companies, Crystal Cream & Butter Company and Crystal Mountain Spring Waters. His portrayal of the “likable guy next door” quickly endeared him to the viewers and he became known as “The Crystal Guy” throughout Northern California.


Jack can also be seen as the host of the Emmy award winning syndicated PBS series, Money Moves, as well as the PBS special series Off-Limits, for which he recently won the prestigious IRIS award from the National Association of Television Program Executives for Outstanding on-camera talent.


When he’s not working, Jack can be found at home in Sacramento, CA with his wife, Jean and sons, Declan and Liam trying to figure out ways to stay off the road.
