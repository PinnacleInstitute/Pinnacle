So, What's the Big Deal About Knowledge?

Prophets throughout all dispensations of time have directed us to absorb as much knowledge as possible. Great leaders from all walks of life stand united in their decree to the human race to continually increase in wisdom and knowledge.

Martin Luther King Jr.
"For Martin Luther King's unique qualities of understanding, compassion and bravery,
 and for his wise and unwavering leadership in securing for all people
 their basic human right to knowledge, dignity and opportunity."

Gandhi
"The real difficulty is that people have no idea of what education truly is.
 We hardly give any thought to the improvement of the character of the educated."

President Gordon B. Hinckley
"We live in a world where knowledge is developing at an ever-accelerating rate.
 Drink deeply from the ever-springing well of wisdom and human experience.
 If you should stop now, you will only stunt your intellectual and spiritual growth."

Henry Ford
" If money is your hope for independence you will never have it.
 The only real security that a man will have in this world
 is a reserve of knowledge, experience, and ability."

Benjamin Franklin
"If a man empties his purse into his head,
 no man can take it away from him.
 An investment in knowledge always pays the best interest."

Helen Keller
" Knowledge is happiness,
 because to have knowledge - broad, deep knowledge -
 is to know true ends from false, and lofty things from low."

Mark Twain 
"There is nothing training cannot do. Nothing is above its reach.
 It can turn bad morals to good; it can destroy bad principles
 and recreate good ones; it can lift men to angelship."

2 Chr. 1: 12
"Wisdom and knowledge is granted unto thee;
 and I will give thee riches, and wealth, and honour,
 such as none of the kings have had that have been before thee,
 neither shall there any after thee have the like."

Prov. 10: 14
"Wise men lay up knowledge …"

Prov. 12: 1
"Whoso loveth instruction loveth knowledge … "

Prov. 18: 15
"The heart of the prudent getteth knowledge ;
 and the ear of the wise seeketh knowledge ."

Prov. 20: 15
"There is gold, and a multitude of rubies:
but the lips of knowledge are a precious a jewel."

Prov. 23: 12
"Apply thine heart unto instruction,
 and thine ears to the words of knowledge ."

Prov. 24: 4
"And by knowledge shall the chambers be filled
 with all precious and pleasant riches."

Prov. 24: 5
"A wise man is strong;
 yea, a man of knowledge increaseth strength."

Isa. 33: 6
"And wisdom and knowledge shall be the stability of thy times…"

2 Pet. 1: 5
"And beside this, giving all diligence,
 add to your faith virtue; and to virtue knowledge;"

D&C 130: 19
"And if a person gains more knowledge and intelligence in this life
 through his diligence and obedience than another,
 he will have so much the advantage in the world to come."
