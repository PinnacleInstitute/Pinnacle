How to Tell if You Have Too Much Debt

Do you find yourself living paycheck to paycheck and not having money for everyday living expenses such as food, utilities, and gas for the car? Do you buy these necessities on credit? Do you find yourself borrowing money to pay bills, or using credit to make it through the holidays? Is there little hope of increasing your income? These are all signs of financial instability and if continued there may be serious consequences.

It has been recommended by the NFCC that a reasonable debt load should be between 15 percent and 20 percent of net income or take home pay. To figure this, simply take your net income and multiply it by .15 or .20. For example, if your family’s monthly net income is $2,000 you could safely afford monthly payments of $400. This does not mean getting into $400 of debt each month, but having your debt payments not exceed this amount. If your monthly debt is below 20 percent you are doing well!

If you believe your debt is severe and you do not feel like you can handle it on your own, seek help from a not-for-profit counseling agency or through Consumer Credit Counseling Services (CCCS). Just as you need to thoroughly investigate a debt consolidation company, you should also investigate any CCCS or nonprofit counseling agency. If you need help locating a non-profit housing and financial counseling service contact the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) at www.hud.gov or call 1-800-569-4287

Take control of your debt, don’t let it control you!
